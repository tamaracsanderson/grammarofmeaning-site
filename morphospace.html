The morphospace: what happens when you let 512 thinkers find their own axes

Every figure is a 6-tuple on a fixed frame. Embed all of them in a geometric space and ask what the space's own structure turns out to be. The answer is the most interesting result we have — and its honest diagnostics are the second-most interesting.

1 · What it is

Each figure carries a coordinate on six categorical axes — epistemic-warrant · ontological-commitment · inferential-operation · telos · stance · ground-world-relation. That's the atom. The morphospace asks the next question: if every position is a point, what shape is the space of positions?

Not a metaphor. A computed embedding, where proximity in the plot must mean proximity in the coding — otherwise the picture is decoration.

2 · The method (and the one we threw away)

Our prototype used PCA on one-hot vectors. A five-cohort round rejected it unanimously: PCA treats nominal categories as continuous, so its distances are meaningless for this data. They also rejected UMAP/t-SNE — prettier clouds, but they distort global distance and aren't reproducible, which is fatal when the claim is "these two are close."

The verdict: Multiple Correspondence Analysis — native to nominal data, corrects for category mass (a rare value isn't drowned by a common one), and yields interpretable axes you can read off the category loadings. Cross-checked against Gower distance + PCoA, which puts the distance metric first. Report Greenacre-adjusted inertias, never naive MCA percentages.

3 · The result — three axes the space found by itself

We did not tell it what to look for. Running MCA on 512 cleanly-coded figures, the geometry recovered three interpretable dimensions:

dim 1 · 47.1% adjusted inertia
Secular ↔ Religious
Loads on material · observation · reductive · epoche — the materialist/empirical/skeptical pole against the religious one. The single largest axis of variation in the entire space.
dim 2 · 22.5%
Personal-theist ↔ Impersonal / Void
Loads on void · dependent-arising · noetic-intuition · identity(non-dual) — the Buddhist/Advaita/impersonal-ground pole against the personal-agentive one.
dim 3 · 10.9%
Kataphatic ↔ Apophatic
Loads on apophatic · mystical-union · contemplation — the negative-theology pole.
The validity signal. Dimension 3 is the apophatic/kataphatic distinction — the same structure our reading work had independently surfaced (canon holds the personal God; mystical reception relocates the essence). The geometry recovered it unsupervised, without being told it existed. A structure that shows up both in close reading and in an unsupervised embedding of 512 coordinates is not an artifact of either.

Together the three dimensions hold 80.5% of the adjusted inertia — most of the space's structure fits in 3D, which is why the space is renderable at all.

4 · The honest diagnostics

The numbers that would let someone contest this:

DiagnosticValueHonest read
adjusted inertia (3D)80.5%Good — most structure is in three dimensions.
MCA vs Gower/PCoA0.696Moderate. The two methods broadly agree on distances — not a tight lock. Acceptable for a scholarly viz; not a strong claim.
held-out O-silhouette0.125Weak-positive. Ontological-commitment contributes to the geometry but doesn't dominate it. The clusters are loose — the space is continuous and fuzzy, not clumped into tidy families.
Read that last row properly, because it cuts against a tidy story. A low silhouette means the traditions do not sit in neat separable clumps. That's consistent with what the coding kept showing — the space is a continuum, positions bleed. A method that reported clean clusters here would be reporting its own wishes.

5 · A data-quality catch worth recording

The first run's dimension 1 was dominated by insufficient-context — the coder's fallback value, not a real position. MCA's mass-correction amplifies rare co-occurring categories, so 13 incompletely-coded figures formed a spurious outlier cluster and hijacked the largest axis (cross-check 0.37; validation failed at 0.035). Dropping those 13 — while keeping not-asserted and unspecified, which are legitimate positions — produced the clean 512-figure result above.

Recorded because it's the kind of thing that silently produces a beautiful, wrong picture.

6 · Occupancy — and the caveat that governs it

The theoretical space is 9 × 13 × 7 × 7 × 6 × 8 = 275,184 coordinates. The corpus occupies 365 distinct ones — 0.13%. The most-occupied single coordinate holds 34 figures (revelation · personal-agentive · exegesis · salvation · reverent · creation — the mainstream-Christian position), which is itself a visible record of corpus skew rather than a finding about religion.

The caveat that governs every occupancy claim: only 536 of 1717 live voices are coded (31%). So this is the geometry of what has been coded, not of what we hold — and certainly not of what exists. An "empty" coordinate is usually a coding gap, not a real absence: two figures we recently proposed acquiring for an empty seat turned out to be sitting in the corpus already, uncoded. Occupancy statistics are statements about our coding progress first, and about the space second.

7 · What is not claimed

Stage 6 = 0. This is a method-validated baseline, not a finding: the embedding method was cohort-validated, the axes are interpretable and honestly reported, and the diagnostics are middling-to-weak in exactly the ways stated above. Multi-label figures will later enter as fuzzy indicators — one weighted point per figure, never two points (which would inflate N and reintroduce the essentialism the frame exists to avoid). The current data is single-label, so this is the clean starting line.
