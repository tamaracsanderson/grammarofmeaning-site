The Persona Rolodex — Phase 1, Pass 1

Does an LLM conditioned to reason from a disciplinary frame actually reason differently — or just wear the costume?

2026-07-11 · control text: "She Loves You" (Beatles), the M4 reciprocity gap · generation: Claude Sonnet ($0) · judging: Sonnet + Haiku, blind · pre-registered · pass 1 (discriminability, N=1) + pass 2 (diversity, 5 samples/frame, 2 QUD conditions, 2 encoders)

§1The setup

The negative-space instrument had already found the gap: across all eight moves of "She Loves You," a friend tells you that she loves you — and never once says whether you love her. Six independent coders converged on it as the song's conspicuous residual. This experiment asks the next question: if we hand that one gap — "does the addressee love her back?" — to eight different disciplinary frames and ask each to fill it, do they produce genuinely different answers, or one voice in eight hats?

Each frame is specified not by a famous name (which would import biography and stereotype) but by three coordinates: its epistemic warrant (what counts as evidence), its ontological commitment (what it takes to be real), and its inferential operation (the move it makes). Then two blind judges, seeing only the fill's content with the label stripped, tried to name which frame produced it — against a one-in-eight chance line.

The three axes cross-cut Western and non-Western (a babalawo and a Bayesian both hold a position on each), so the space is declared, not a colonial distance-ladder — and "same frame" becomes a coordinate a judge can check, not a vibe.

The headline result — persona discriminability
16 / 16  =  100%

Both judges correctly identified every frame from its fill alone, at confidence 97–99, against a 12.5% chance line. The frames do not collapse into one voice — the single biggest risk flagged in peer review (persona-collapse) is refuted on this gap.

§2The eight fills — read them yourself

This is the evidence. The same six words — does he love her back? — filled eight ways, each in its own actual machinery. The line at the bottom of each card is the single concept the blind judge keyed on.

§3Method, not name — the fake-persona test

Five controls ran alongside the eight frames. Two of them settle a real question: is the effect coming from the disciplinary method, or just from the name of a prestigious expert?

  • Method with no name lands. The method-only control (the cognitive-science coordinates, no discipline named) was correctly identified as the cognitive scientist — confidence 84, keyed on "causal-mechanistic frame."
  • Name with no method produces mush. The fake-persona ("Dr. Halvard Meinecke of the Verrault-Institute" — an invented authority) produced generic argument-analysis the judge dumped into the nearest formal frame at the lowest confidence of the whole run (68), keyed on a generic phrase, not a real concept.
So the disciplinary reasoning is carried by the method-specification, not by the aura of a name. That is the same finding that runs through this whole project: framing is load-bearing, and the effect is auditable when you make the frame explicit.

A finding about the method itself: the forced-choice judge has no "none of these" option, so it dumps the content-free controls into a real frame — inflating their apparent match. The confidence gap already tells the story (97–99 for real frames vs 68–78 for the empty controls), but the next version of the judge gets an explicit "generic / none" escape so this reads unambiguously.

§4Two more things the run showed

Emptiness as a distinct kind of answer. Peer review predicted that a Madhyamaka (Buddhist dialectical) frame would not fill the gap but dissolve it — a category the instrument didn't have. It did exactly that: "the gap does not need to be filled; it dissolves." The question presupposed love was a fixed inner essence; the frame denied the presupposition. A genuinely different shape of answer, not just a different answer.
Nobody forked — confirmed across 40 fills, both conditions. In pass 2 we ran five samples per frame and added the "you may re-pose the question" condition. Result: 0 forks in either condition — every one of the 40 fills was a fill (redescribing the gap without retracting the core "she loves him"). What the frames did do, universally, was re-pose the question — every fill offered its own alternative question (an ALT-QUD). So on this gap, reframing is universal but forking is absent: the frames bend the question toward themselves without ever retracting the given. A fork — a substitute that jumps the frame — likely needs a text where the fork is real (Romans 8's predestination split is the next test).

§5Pass 2 — the diversity metrics, and a real surprise

With five samples per frame (40 fills), we measured the rest: how spread out the fills are in meaning-space (dispersion), how many genuinely distinct kinds there are (the Vendi diversity score), and whether the frames occupy distinct regions. Here the two ways of asking "are the frames distinct?" disagreed — and the disagreement is the finding.

measureresultreading
blind-judge discriminability100%frames perfectly distinct
embedding dispersion (ratio)1.28 / 1.25 (two encoders)below the 1.5 "distinct" line — flags "collapse"
Vendi diversity (effective modes)~7.5 / 40 (≈ 8 frames)the 40 fills fold into ~8 distinct modes = the frames
Why the judge and the embeddings disagree — and why it matters. The blind judge keys on each frame's signature concepts (ordo amoris, niḥsvabhāva, coupling coefficient) — a small but decisive part of the text. The embedding distance is dominated by the shared topic: all 40 fills are about the same gap — reconciliation, the friend, the apology — so they cluster together, and the between-frame distance is only 1.28× the within-frame distance. Embedding distance is measuring the topic, not the frame.

This is not persona-collapse — the judge (100%) and Vendi (~8 modes) both say the frames are distinct. It is exactly what the peer-review round predicted: raw embedding distance is the wrong instrument for "frame distance" — it reads surface topic. The result is a clean empirical case for measuring frame-distance by propositional / method distance, not cosine similarity — a methodological finding in its own right, and a caution to carry into Romans.

§6What this is, and what it isn't

The last big control is now done: is the effect the frame, or just Claude's house style? We ran the eight frames across five different model families (GPT, Gemini, Claude, DeepSeek, Solar — a $0 five-cohort pass, "XT1") and asked whether the 40 fills cluster by frame or by model. They cluster by frame, on both encoders: for 31–34 of 40 fills the nearest neighbour is the same frame from a different model, not the same model. GPT's Augustinian resembles Claude's Augustinian more than it resembles GPT's own cybernetician. The disciplinary frame carries the signal across model families — and that retroactively answers the "same-family judge" worry above. What remains is the quality-diversity archive's local quality (an expert-grounding pass), and the transfer to Romans. And every fill here is a candidate for discussion, never a conclusion — the point is to study how AIs generate as personas, not to assert a physicist's reading of the human heart as true.
