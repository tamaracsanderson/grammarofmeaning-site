Grammar schema v4 — probe-aware (exploration fork)

forked from the v3 canonical (romans_grammar_schema_probe_aware_v2) · researcher exploration · 2026-06-17 · a study aid (a standpoint, not a totalized universal — Q64/Q66).

What is this?
A study aid for reading how one idea — Paul's Romans 8:28–30 — changed as it passed through thinkers across the centuries. It maps the moves by which meaning gets remade.
Why?
To test a question: do different traditions converge on the same moves of meaning-making, even when their beliefs disagree? The structure converges; the beliefs diverge. (A question we're testing — not yet a finding.)
The 7 questions we ask of every thinker — these are the "lenses" below

    At what level are we analyzing? Every move is anchored down to a text-chunk (the evidence) and situated up in a context (the zoom-out). The same chunk anchors moves at different grains — Lovejoy's universe→planet→earth zoom (Q43/Q53). Pick a grain; the move re-centers, the chunk-anchor stays constant.

    ⚠ The context tier is always shown, never optional: a move with no zoom-out reads as a half-coded node — and the missing zoom-out is the decontextualized-verse misreading (§2.8 bias-control). Grain labels (verse/pericope/argument/letter) are Romans-specific; the grain vocabulary generalizes when the schema covers non-biblical sources. Candidate model (§2.1), not locked.
