What this is. The move-grammar is a directed graph; a directed graph runs both ways. We have only ever run it forward (a text → its readings → reception). This is the first pilot of the backward direction: take a later doctrinal formula, decompose its moves, and regress each move back through the interpreters who made it to the NT locus — then down to the Jewish and Greco-Roman roots the NT itself grafts from. This is the same operation Saquib runs tracing a Facebook post back to its origin: provenance-tracing over a citation graph.

The anti-teleology rule. Backward and forward were composed independently and joined only at the pivot (Rom 8:28–30). We did not build a forward bridge just because we knew where it had to arrive. Backward regression has failure modes forward never tested — fixed-endpoint teleology is structural here, and pagan-root grafts are the most hallucination-prone edges of all. Those risks are the point of the review box, next.

⚑ What needs your review

Every speculative link and every judgment call, listed so nothing hides as settled genealogy. Ordered most-fragile first.

  1. reconstructed · graft Stoic pronoia → Rom 8:28 ("all things work together," πάντα συνεργεῖ). Highest-risk edge in the whole tree. Is Paul grafting Stoic cosmic providence, or just using shared Hellenistic vocabulary? No citation exists — this is a coherent hypothesis, drawn dashed. Should it appear at all?
  2. reconstructed · graft Philo / Middle-Platonic mediation → the pivot. Placed as a possible conduit between Greek concepts and Paul. Entirely inferential; no textual link. Keep, demote, or cut?
  3. reconstructed · parallel Qumran 1QS "Two Spirits" (III–IV) → the chain's determinism. A real Second-Temple predestinarian parallel, but Paul never quotes Qumran. Is this genealogy or shared Zeitgeist? Marked parallel, not citation.
  4. attested? · judgment Hebrew yāda‘ (covenantal "knowing," Amos 3:2; Jer 1:5) → "foreknew" (προέγνω). Marked attested on lexical-semantic grounds (LXX renders yāda‘ with ginōskō). But is it a root the verb grafts, or just semantic background? Provenance-class is a judgment call.
  5. judgment Choice of doctrine to regress. I started the backward run from the Reformed formula (Dort's Unconditional Election). That is one fork; I could have regressed the Arminian one. Should the pilot regress both forks symmetrically to avoid pre-loading the Reformed reading?
  6. judgment Interpreter selection. Augustine · Calvin · Arminius · Wesley — four of many. Gottschalk, Aquinas, Beza, Barth are omitted. The mediating path doctrine→interpreter→verse is only as honest as this roster.
  7. judgment The proginōskō fork (G4) as THE hinge. The whole left/right split rides on one verb's sense (fore-love vs fore-see-faith). Is that over-loading a single word?
  8. structural Do roots belong "below" the NT at all? Drawing jewish/pagan roots beneath the pivot visually asserts a downward genealogy. That framing may itself reify a lineage the texts don't license.
  9. unvalidated G4–G7 obligation-marks are the analyst's. "This absence is obligated" is my judgment, carried verbatim from the worked doc, untested at IRR-B (IRR166) and at coding. Treat as claims to be adjudicated, not results.
  10. provenance Interpreter → pivot edges are drawn attested. Augustine, Calvin, Arminius, and Wesley each explicitly comment on Rom 8:28–30, so these are solid — but "attested that they read it" is not "attested that their reading is the verse's sense." The solid line means the citation exists, not that it is correct.
