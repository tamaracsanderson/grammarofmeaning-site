The coordinate space — 365 of 275,184

Every framing of meaning is a point in a six-axis space — how it knows, what it takes to be real, how it moves, what it is for, its posture, its world-relation. The space has 275,184 coherent coordinates. The corpus, so far, has posed 365 of them. The generator spins that space: land on an occupied point and meet the minds who took it; land on an empty one and read a question no one has asked.

Spin the space

Gaps are generated by moving one axis off a coordinate a real thinker occupies — so every empty point shown sits one step from a real mind, not floating in incoherent space. (Per reading-SB: spin near occupied regions first; not every empty cell is coherent.)

The spin weights — the real distribution

The generator weights each axis by how the 512 figures actually distribute. That distribution is not neutral: the corpus is Abrahamic-heavy, and the weights show it. We surface the skew rather than flatten it — the bias is the data, and hiding it would be the error (§2.8).

Read the red bars as bias, not truth. personal-agentive fills 232 of 512 because the corpus over-represents the traditions that wrote the most and were digitized first — not because that ontology is more correct. The empty coordinates are disproportionately the under-written ones; the generator’s job is to make them visible.
