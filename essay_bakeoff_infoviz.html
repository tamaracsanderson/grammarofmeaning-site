How a Grammar of Meaning essay actually gets made

The engine gathers and drafts. The human makes the essay. Below, each step is drawn at the size of the real work it takes — so where the labor actually lives is obvious at a glance.

Design rationale. One disciplined metaphor — a river the human carves. The amber channel is human judgment; the slate tributaries are the engine feeding in. The single load-bearing encoding: each step's area is proportional to real effort, so Revision (~22 passes — where the essay is made) dwarfs every automated step. The eye reaches the honest conclusion before reading a word: the machine drafts; the person does the writing. High data-ink; flow left→right; the legend's bar-heights double as the effort key.

Worked example — Rabiʿa al-ʿAdawiyya, “What’s Worth Wanting”
Burn heaven, douse hell — so what remains is love, free and clear.  (illustrative phrasing; not a locked citation — verify before it goes public)
human · 1
Seed
Day 3 of the simplicity series. Rabiʿa — whose reason for wanting less is the strangest: love God for nothing, not for paradise, not from fear of hell.
engine · 2
Harvest
Pulls the 8th-c. Basra sitz (canal-port, slave economy, the Muʿtazila debates) and the ʿabd etymology — the worshipper is literally “slave of God.” ★ a freed slave runs the streets with fire in one hand, water in the other.
human · 3
Form
Chooses the Thurman flex: invocation → story → circle → hand back. The default self-help “audit / now do this” march is cut; her own prayer carries the close. Proclaim, don’t prescribe.
engine · 4
Draft
Several doors drafted at once — the torch invocation, the world-first open, the freed-slave hinge. The author grafts; she never just picks one.
engine · 5
Review (9-panel)
Adversarial panel + honesty rail: flag the torch-and-bucket as legend (“the tradition tells”), not record; keep her emptying distinct from Francis’s love — don’t flatten one move across voices.
engine · 6
Rebalance
Corpus skew made visible (§2.8): a Sufi woman foregrounded, not buried under the Abrahamic-heavy default spread. Include and tag — never defer.
human · 7 · the bulk
Revision — ~22 passes
Where the essay is actually made. The ʿabd/slave hinge; the two-question close (room + intrinsic love); the prayer reframed as her answer; the al-Ghazālī / Smith provenance; Basra made imaginable. Dozens of line-level passes — by far the most time of any step. This is the writing.
human · 8
Footnotes
Pin every claim to a named source — e.g. the prayer → Margaret Smith, Rabiʿa the Mystic (1928), not just “ʿAttar.” The “where this came from” section: generosity to sources.
output · 9
Publish
“Whatever is still standing when both have gone out — that is the thing you actually loved.”

Framing. What carries across Rabiʿa, Diogenes, and Francis is a move — “want less” — sung in a different voice each time (freedom · God · love). It is the same move in a different voice: an analogy you can carry into your own life, not a claim that the traditions are secretly one (perennialism) or hermetically separate (particularism).
