The argument · ~10 minutes

How meaning gets made, and how to measure it

A method for reading what a text does, what it leaves unsaid, and what a different mind could have said instead — worked end to end on one song. The apparatus behind every claim here lives in the reference; this page is the argument.

Status: a validated method producing candidate output. The corpus coding has not run (Stage 6 = 0). Nothing below is a finding about religion. What is claimed is that the instrument works and that its failures are legible — the open debt is summed at the end, not scattered.
1

A text is a sequence of moves, not a sequence of sentences

Take the Beatles' She Loves You — chosen because it is short, secular, and nobody's scripture, so the method can be checked without reverence getting in the way.

Read as sentences it is a boy telling another boy some news. Read as moves — one operation performed by an agent on something — it is eight distinct acts. Someone hurt her. She suffered. She reappraised. She loves you. You fear the loss. The friend relays it. He exhorts you to be glad, then to act.

The unit matters because it is what makes two texts comparable. Sentences do not travel across languages, genres or centuries; operations do. "She reappraised him" and a Confucian text's account of revised judgement are the same operation with different furniture — and that, not shared vocabulary, is what a comparison would have to be built on.

Coding a move records who acts, on what, and by what warrant. It also records the edges between moves: which move feeds the next, which fills an earlier gap, which composes into the whole. In this song those edges are lopsided in a way worth noticing — seven feed forward, but four run backward, and all four land on the same move: she loves you. Five of the eight moves exist partly to answer it. That is a structural fact about the text, not an impression of it.

2

Then code what the text does not say

This is the part that is unusual, and it is the reason for the method.

Every move leaves things open. She loves you does not say why she loves you, how much, whether you love her back, or how the friend knows. Those are not omissions in the sense of sloppiness; they are structured silences — the text's own shape determines which of them it can close and which it cannot.

So each gap is coded on two questions: is it opaque (the text genuinely does not supply it) and is it in focus (the text draws attention to it)? A gap that is both is where the interesting work is. Then: can the text close it from inside itself?

Run the song's central move through that and the answer is mostly yes. How much is answered by her suffering. How the friend knows is answered by his having met her. Roughly four-fifths of what the move leaves open, the other seven moves quietly supply. The song is very nearly a self-completing object.

What it cannot close is the finding. Three gaps survive: whether he loves her back — never stated; why the friend cares — his stake is never given; and the positive ground of her love — the song removes an obstacle to it without ever supplying a reason for it. Those are not our observations about the song. They are what the form produces, and they are exactly where a later reader would have to step in.

One correction worth keeping visible. The first coding of this song was done by the same author who built the method, and it named a fourth residual — the woman never speaks. Five independent coders, given the same eight moves and no context, did not find it: one of five. They found the addressee's silence instead. The un-voiced woman is a real reading, but it is a reader's lens, not a gap the form produces — and the primed author could not tell the difference. The measurement corrected the author; that is the mechanism working, so it ships visible.

3

Change one move, and generate the worlds that follow

If the method has understood the text, it should be able to say what a different text would have had to do. So: change one move at the root and walk forward.

you hurt her becomes you healed her — the contrary. Everything downstream is then regenerated, position by position, and the question at each fork is not "what could happen next?" but "what does this song's own structure require here, in a world where the wound was never made?"

At every fork after the root the engine makes exactly five attempts: one control that changes nothing, and four perturbations that each move a single dimension of the mind doing the talking and hold the rest still. The dimension is chosen systematically; only its new value is the model's invention. Distance is never requested — asking a model for "something further away" measures its obedience, not the thing. Distance is measured afterwards, from the result.

The control is not a spare option. Without it every path is forced to drift a little at every step, and the world in which nothing about the mind needed to change becomes unreachable — which would be an artifact of the generator, not a fact about the text.

What comes back is a fan of alternative worlds. In one, the healed woman becomes reliant, the healer feels trapped, withdraws, and inflicts a new wound — and the song's apology arrives after all, for a different injury. In another, she reads Friedan, outgrows the couple, and leaves: no apology is available, because no injury was done. Same substitution, different ending.

4

Most of what it generates is wrong — the gates say how

A generator that produces only plausible continuations has been tuned until it looks good, and has therefore stopped being evidence. This one keeps what it rejects, and sorts it.

Four checks run, and they do four different jobs — which is why calling them all "gates" misleads:

  • Era. Was the governing idea available in 1963? "She started antidepressants" is coherent and unborn. Prunes.
  • State. Does the move contradict what already happened on this path? After a healing, a move that needs the wound still open does not follow. Prunes.
  • Structure. Does it keep the relation the source had at this position? This shapes what gets generated and removes nothing.
  • Worldview. How far is this from the mind the song was speaking from? It removes nothing at all — it only measures. A ruler, not a filter.

What fails sorts into two piles, and keeping them apart is the whole point. One says a different world could hold this — the move is coherent, its idea simply was not available here. The other says this does not follow in any world. One is a boundary of the era; the other is a boundary of coherence. Collapse them and the doorways go out with the nonsense.

Because the first pile is not garbage — it is the most interesting output the method produces. A branch barred because its idea belongs to Sufi metamorphosis is pointing at a tradition, from inside a pop song, without anyone having asked it to.

5

"How far" needs a space to be far in

The worldview check is doing something the other three are not: it compares minds, not moves. That requires minds to be specifiable objects rather than names.

So a mind is described by its commitments — how it knows, what it takes to be real, how it reasons, what it is for, how it regards its object, what it does with a text, how it relates ground to world. Seven dimensions. A choice on each is a coordinate, and a coordinate is a possible mind, whether or not anyone ever held it. The space of all of them is the morphospace — a term borrowed from theoretical morphology, where it names the space of shapes an organism could take, and where the empty regions are treated as real.

That is what makes the earlier question answerable. "Has this drifted out of the song's worldview" is a distance between two coordinates — nothing more mysterious than counting how many of the seven commitments have changed. And it buys three things a name cannot: one thinker can occupy several coordinates as they change their mind; two thinkers who share one are measurably near each other; and a coordinate can be coherent with nobody standing on it.

That last case is the point of the whole apparatus. An unoccupied but coherent position is either a mind nobody has held — or a gap in what we have read. The method cannot yet tell those apart, and saying which is which is the work.

6

What is not shown

Summed here rather than distributed, so it can be read in one go.

The comparative claim is unexercised. Every cross-text relation — rhyme, cousin, citation, and the contact-check that would rule out borrowing — has zero coded instances. The instrument is built for comparison and has not yet made one. This is the largest gap and it is not a technicality.

Reliability is moderate and pending. There is no clean inter-rater figure yet. The multi-coder rounds have corrected the author more than once, which is evidence the check works, not evidence the coding is settled.

The corpus coding has not run. Stage 6 = 0. Figure-level coding exists on a small roster; passage-level coding does not exist.

Three axes were settled by judgement, not diagnostics. Four of the seven passed empirical separation tests. Three did not — they were resolved in a lock-cycle, which is a weaker warrant, and is recorded as one.

Roughly forty smaller open items are flagged in place through the reference. They are listed, not hidden; the count is given here so the total is not something a reader has to assemble themselves.

The claim of this page is narrow and, I think, defensible: the instrument reads a text, finds what it cannot say, generates what it might have said instead, and can tell the difference between an alternative that is merely distant and one that belongs to somebody else's world. Whether that produces anything true about religion is a question this page does not answer, and does not pretend to.
