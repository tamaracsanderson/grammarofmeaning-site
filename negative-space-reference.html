1 — the move Q1: the 7 grammar, H0–H2, narrator

A move = one operation and its arguments — what the text does, not what it says. It has CORE-7 slots in three H-layers (from Role-and-Reference Grammar's layered clause):

H0  NARRATOR      — the FRAME layer. who tells it / from what point of view. (was "warrant", Decision 161)
H1  PROPOSITION   — the ASSERT layer. the move's content: operation · agent · substrate · outcome.
H2  MODIFIERS     — the QUALIFY layer. two riders on H1 slots: type (rides operation) · voice (rides agent).
M = Narrator[ Operationtype ( Agentvoice , Substrate ) → Outcome ]

Read aloud: a narrator frames an operation (of a certain type) by which an agent (in a certain voice) acts on a substrate to produce an outcome. M is the whole bracket, not just the agent.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  H0  NARRATOR  ── frames ──►  "the friend, as eyewitness-messenger"   │  who tells it, on what authority
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  H1  PROPOSITION                                                     │
│        operation ── the move ──►  love                               │  the verb
│      (H2 type)  rides operation ──►  report / deem / transform …     │  which KIND of move
│        agent  ── originator ──►  she                                 │  who does it (in-frame, not the author)
│      (H2 voice)  rides agent ──►  active / middle-passive / null     │  the agency-mode
│        substrate ── acts on ──►  you                                 │  the input / given
│        outcome ── produces ──►  (the bond love yields)               │  the output / result

Why type & voice are H2. H1 is what is asserted; H2 is how it's qualified. "love" is asserted; that it's a report and in the active voice qualifies it. narrator (was "warrant") moved to H0 because the authority-source frames the whole proposition from above — it isn't inside it. "consequence" is removed: an outcome only becomes a consequence when it's the substrate of a later move, i.e. outcome(N)=substrate(N+1) — a production edge, not a slot.

2 — surrounding Q2: sitz + paradigm

Two fields shared across a whole move (fill once). Different axes — don't merge.

FieldDefinitionAxisExample (M4)
sitzthe historical SITUATION OF THE TELLING — where/when/how-the-world-wasthe teller's worlda 1963 pop single; a friend brokering a reconciliation
paradigmthe conceptual FRAME the CONTENT assumes — the governing worldviewthe content's worldromantic-love-as-reconcilable

Mnemonic: the narrator sits in a sitz; the content sits in a paradigm. (Romans: Paul's sitz = c.57 CE, Jew–Gentile house-churches; the paradigm = providence / election.)

3 — a move IS a frame a moment in time

Each move is one freeze-frame: one operation, fixed agent/substrate/outcome, one narrator, one sitz. The text is a sequence of frames (M1→M8). So:

  • time lives in the edges, not the slots — a slot is a still; the production edge (outcome(N)→substrate(N+1)) is the motion between stills.
  • "beyond-frame" means literally outside this freeze-frame's declared world — a value the frame can't hold.
  • every frame has three horizons, because three minds stand at different times: the agent (in the frame), the narrator (telling it), the reader (receiving it, maybe centuries later) = the 3 seers.

4 — the detection grid Q4: 2×2 × seer

Every position (a slot OR a frame-role) is placed on a 2×2: visibility × salience.

AXIS 1 — VISIBILITY (binary):  transparent | opaque
         = is the value recoverable from the text? closed-class (tense/mood) → transparent;
           open-class (which love? how much?) → opaque unless a modifier states it.
AXIS 2 — SALIENCE (binary):  in-focus | out-of-focus
         = would filling it MATERIALLY change what the addressee is positioned to
           believe/feel/do, relative to the FIXED QUD?

                    in-focus (matters)          out-of-focus (trivial)
    transparent   ┌────────────────────────┬────────────────────────┐
    (recoverable) │       GRAMMAR          │       TRIVIAL          │
                  │  said & load-bearing   │  said but irrelevant   │
    ──────────────┼────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
    opaque        │   ★ THE GAP ★          │       NOISE            │
    (unrecover-   │  the money cell —      │  unrecoverable but     │
    able)         │  unsaid AND it matters │  doesn't matter        │
                  └────────────────────────┴────────────────────────┘

The seer lens (× seer). Once a position is in THE GAP, ask "opaque to WHOM?" — evidential, not psychological:

SeerQuestionSeer? Seen?
agentdoes she (in-frame) know it?both a seer AND one of the seen (a slot)
narratorcan the teller vouch for it?both a seer AND seen (H0 slot)
readercan we decode it from the text?seer only

4·E — slot-gaps vs edge-gaps the second kind of gap

§4's grid finds gaps inside a move. But a gap can also be a missing relation BETWEEN moves — an arrow the text never draws — and the grid can't see those. Two kinds of gap, two detectors:

KindWhat's missingDetected byExample
slot-gapan argument within one move — the money cellthe slot-detector — §4's 2×2 grid, run on each moveM4: does his love exist? (the Reciprocity role)
edge-gapa relation between moves the text leaves undrawnthe edge-detector — a missing-relation pass across moves (not the grid)the reciprocity arrow: her love (M4) → your apology (M7–8) leaves your love unstated
Proven distinct. The Beatles reciprocity gap scored 1/8 under slot-detection but ~8/8 under edge-detection — the same gap is nearly invisible to one instrument and loud to the other, so they are not the same instrument. The finding transferred to Romans (the human-love ↔ chain arrow). Don't confuse an edge-gap with a slot-gap (a missing detail inside a move) or a narrative alternative (a different plot).
BARRED is a joint property — coordinate × gap. A gap's door (§10) isn't fixed by the gap alone; it depends on the coordinate × gap pair. The same material-frame mind FILLs "why do we suffer?" (mechanism) but is BARRED from "whom did God foreknow?" (there is no immaterial foreknower it can posit). Read the door off both together.

See the moves + edges drawn out → the M1–M8 edge diagram; the branch structure → the move-tree.

5 — said / unsaid / alternative Q5: still in use?

Yes — three DIFFERENT things; keep them apart (Decision 159):

TermWhat it isWhere it lives
SAIDthe value actually present in the slotthe CORE-7 grammar (the positive coding)
UNSAIDa value ABSENT but in-frame; detected + flavoredthe grid + flavors (the negative coding)
ALTERNATIVEa specific competing value that could have filled the slot insteadthe alternative axis (the generation input)

6 — the alternative axis Q6: contrary / null / the frame-jump

For any slot, the values that could have gone there sort into four kinds by their relation to the SAID:

SAID = "loves"
  ├─ CONTRARY      = the far pole; same voice + agent, opposite valence   → "hates"       (still engaged)
  ├─ [BETWEEN]     = the bounded spectrum of other nameable values        → "likes / misses"
  ├─ PRIVATION     = the agent/voice WITHDRAWN — the emptied slot         → "is indifferent to"  (the null; origin=0)
  └─ BEYOND-FRAME  = outside the declared universe — the un-imagined      → (flag, don't code)
Null & the frame-jump (your exact question). PRIVATION = the null — the slot emptied to zero. "Indifference" is the null of "love" (the origin of the number-line, not its negative pole — which is why the opposite of love is indifference, not hate). And "she hates you" JUMPS THE FRAME: swapping SAID "loves" → CONTRARY "hates" is a SUBSTITUTE — it retracts the love (you can't say "she loves you AND hates you" as one move). A substitute voids the premise, so it doesn't complete this frame; it starts a different one (a fork). Contrary/substitute = the door OUT of the frame; fill = the door that stays IN.

6·K — the two branch-kinds narrative vs interpretive — tag, don't conflate

When you enumerate what a move could do next (§6), each branch is one of two kinds. Tag each; keep both; never merge them.

KindWhat variesExample
[narrative]an alternative way the story / history could go — a contingent eventhow she reacted; how a tradition spread; who held power. Scripture is full of these.
[interpretive]an alternative reading of the same words — a reading divergencesingle vs double predestination on one verse
Different but not separate. Narrative history shapes interpretation — so keep both branches, rendered distinctly (different colours), never collapsed into one tree. Don't confuse a plot-alternative with an interpretive fork: that exact conflation over-generated the song's move-tree in the pilot (a story-branch masquerading as a reading-branch). See both kinds rendered → the move-tree.

7 — the gap scan Q7: all 7 parts, or only where not explicit?

You scan all 7 slots (+ frame-roles), but a slot only PRODUCES a gap when it lands in THE GAP cell (opaque × in-focus). Most land in GRAMMAR or TRIVIAL — those are not gaps.

Your M2 example is exactly right. In she suffered badly: agent="she" and the fact-of-suffering are transparent+in-focus → GRAMMAR. Only how much she suffered is opaque+in-focus → 1 GAP, on the operation's Degree role, flavored underdetermined, a FILL that gets filled-internally by M4's degree. One move, one gap — the norm. M4 (the money cell) has ~6.

8 — frame-roles Q8: what degree, reason are

Beyond the CORE-7, every operation opens frame-roles (FrameNet — the slots the verb implies). Most gaps sit here:

Frame-roleAsks"suffer""love"
Degreehow much?how badly she sufferedhow much she loves you
Reasonwhy?why she sufferedwhy she loves you
Mannerin what way?how it showedhow the love shows
Reciprocitymutual?—does he love her back

Plus a universal discourse layer on every move (temporality · evidentiality · modality · reciprocity · interiority). A gap can sit on a CORE-7 slot OR a frame-role OR a discourse field.

the frame's coordinate — 5 axes, not 3 E×O×M + telos + stance

A frame's persona (§8, and the slot machine) is a coordinate, not a name — a point in an axis-space, generative between named landmarks. The axis-set is now five:

E  epistemic warrant  — how it knows     revelation · reason · observation · practice · testimony · divination
O  ontology           — what's real      transcendent-personal · relational · material · impersonal-process
M  method             — how it reasons   exegesis · derivation · causal-explanation · dialectical-negation · analogical-mapping
T  telos      ● green      — what it's FOR    contemplation · flourishing · harmony · knowledge-understanding · liberation · power-mastery · salvation
S  stance     ○ candidate  — posture to gap   charitable · reverent · suspicious · reductive · reparative · paranoid

telos is IRR-locked (green, 5/5 · IRR183); stance is a candidate — held for its mini-round, not finalized. Only green- or candidate-labelled axis-values go on the public reels (§2.1 — no premature anchoring); the figure_exom atlas records all five, and a cell's door is read as coordinate × gap (above). This is a light note — the negative-space page is the move-grammar reference; the full E×O×M + telos + stance treatment belongs on a dedicated coordinate/persona reference.

9 & 12 — resolution edges · reception · why RESIDUAL is caps

Every detected gap resolves one of three ways:

ResolutionDefinitionAn edge?Example (M4)
filled-internallyanother move in THIS text answers ityes — a completion edge (can run backward = suspense)M4 warrant ← M6 (the witness)
generatablethe text doesn't, but the fill engine can produce an in-frame filla candidate edgeM4 degree → "a deep, enduring love"
RESIDUALneither text nor monotonic fill closes it — only reception (new eyes, later) canyes — the reception edge (points OUT of the text)the friend's motive; why she loves you
The 400-years-later theologian is the reception edge made literal. A RESIDUAL in Paul ("God foreknew whom?") is exactly the gap Augustine fills 400 years later, and Arminius fills differently 1500 years later. The text leaves it residual; reception is the set of later moves, by later agents, that close it — and because they close it with different fills, they fork. The residuals are where the cross-tradition thesis (Q2) lives.

Why RESIDUAL is caps: it's the headline output — the flag for exactly where history has to step in (the reception target, the fork site, the convergence-test point). Same field, marked.

10 & 11 — the door: fill / substitute / barred the "— (foreclosed)" fix

You caught a real bug. The door is a three-value field, not two:

ValueTestMeaningMonotonic?
FILL"…AND X" co-holdsX completes the move without retracting ityes → stays in frame
SUBSTITUTE"…X INSTEAD" retractsX replaces the SAID, voiding the premiseno → jumps frame
BARREDcan't even propose Xthe move forecloses the knowledge — the coder is blockedn/a — only reception opens it

So — (foreclosed) should read BARRED. BARRED always co-occurs with the foreclosed or horizon-limit flavor. And the grid & flavors are two layers run in order: A the grid (binary — WHERE is the gap?) → B the door (FILL/SUBSTITUTE/BARRED — CAN it close?) → C the flavor (graded — WHY is it opaque?). The flavors live downstream of the grid, not inside a cell — which keeps the grid's κ tight (binary) while the graded flavor underdetermined (= translucent = a range not a point) carries the honest continuum.

Extensible (FIT-OR-EXTEND). The 6 = the current census, not a lockbox. A new pattern earns a new flavor only with a distinct detection-method (else it's a relabel), IRR-validated. The grid detects (fixed); the flavors grow.

13 — the generator do we have an engine yet?

Not yet — the honest status:

EngineJobStatus
detector (IRR177)names the absence — which gaps, cell, flavor✅ done + reliability-locked
SUBSTITUTE enginegenerates the forks (the door OUT)⚙️ exists (negative_space_generator_v1.py), needs the salience filter
FILL engine (money cell)generates what-could-be still in frame — the branches not taken📝 spec'd, NOT built — the next build after the pilot
Detection vs generation: IRR177 = detection (naming the absence). The generator = generation (making what could-be / could-have-been that's still in the frame = the branches not taken). The generator is the theology payoff: on "God foreknew," the FILL engine articulates the in-frame fills the text leaves open, the SUBSTITUTE engine articulates the forks (single vs double predestination), and reception (Augustine, Arminius) shows which branch each tradition took. Convergence across traditions (Q2) is the rhyme across those picks.
