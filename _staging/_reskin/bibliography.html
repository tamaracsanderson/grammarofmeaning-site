Reference · the scholarly editions the work builds on

Bibliography

Exactly which edition to pull — disambiguated by translator + series.

How to read this bibliography: for older texts, the translator + series disambiguates, not the title. Search the bold search string; the edition line tells you which hit to take. ⚠ = confident on translator / series, less so on exact reprint-year — any recent reprint of that translation is fine. ✅ FREE = public domain, also readable online (a library catalogue lookup is optional).

⚠ translator / series confident · reprint-year approximate ✅ FREE public domain · readable online
00Tier 0 · the three lenses (get these first)
  • Lindbeck, The Nature of Doctrine — George A. Lindbeck, The Nature of Doctrine: Religion and Theology in a Postliberal Age, Westminster John Knox Press, 25th Anniversary ed., 2009 (new intro by Bruce Marshall; the 1984 Westminster Press orig is also fine).
    → HOLLIS: "Lindbeck Nature of Doctrine"
  • Koselleck, Futures Past — Reinhart Koselleck, Futures Past: On the Semantics of Historical Time, trans. Keith Tribe, Columbia University Press, 2004 (same Tribe translation as the MIT Press 1985).
    → HOLLIS: "Koselleck Futures Past Tribe" (⚠ confirm your OA copy is the Tribe translation, not the German).
  • Ricoeur, The Symbolism of Evil — Paul Ricoeur, trans. Emerson Buchanan, Beacon Press, 1967 (the "second naïveté" is in the Conclusion).
    → HOLLIS: "Ricoeur Symbolism of Evil Buchanan"
01Tier 1a · the theology spine ✅ FREE — public domain (library only if you want print)
  • Augustine, Ad Simplicianum — the one that pulls junk. Take it in: Augustine: Earlier Writings, ed./trans. John H. S. Burleigh, Library of Christian Classics vol. VI, Westminster Press, 1953 (contains "To Simplician—On Various Questions").
    → HOLLIS: "Augustine Earlier Writings Burleigh Library of Christian Classics"
  • Augustine, anti-Pelagian (Grace / Free Will / Predestination of the Saints / Rebuke & Grace) — modern: Answer to the Pelagians, New City Press, Works of Saint Augustine (WSA) I/23–26; ✅ FREE older: Nicene & Post-Nicene Fathers (NPNF) 1st series, vol. 5 (ccel.org).
    → HOLLIS: "Augustine Answer to the Pelagians New City Press"
  • Calvin, Institutes III — John Calvin, Institutes of the Christian Religion, ed. John T. McNeill, trans. Ford Lewis Battles, Library of Christian Classics vols XX–XXI, Westminster Press, 1960. (THE scholarly English — avoid the older Beveridge for citation.)
    → HOLLIS: "Calvin Institutes McNeill Battles"
  • Aquinas, Summa I q.23 — bilingual scholarly: Summa Theologiae, Blackfriars Latin-English ed. (predestination ≈ vol. 5); ✅ FREE: English Dominican Province trans. (newadvent.org).
    → HOLLIS: "Aquinas Summa Theologiae Blackfriars"
  • Luther, Bondage of the Will — trans. J. I. Packer & O. R. Johnston, Revell, 1957; or Luther's Works (American Edition) vol. 33, Fortress.
    → HOLLIS: "Luther Bondage of the Will Packer Johnston"
  • Luther, Lectures on Romans — trans. Wilhelm Pauck, Library of Christian Classics vol. XV, Westminster, 1961; or Luther's Works vol. 25, Fortress.
    → HOLLIS: "Luther Lectures on Romans Pauck"
  • Wesley, "Predestination Calmly Considered" — in The Works of John Wesley, Bicentennial Edition, Abingdon Press (the scholarly one; older Jackson ed. vol. 10 also fine); ✅ FREE online (Wesley Center).
    → HOLLIS: "Works of John Wesley Bicentennial Abingdon"
  • Edwards, Freedom of the Will — Jonathan Edwards, ed. Paul Ramsey, Works of Jonathan Edwards vol. 1, Yale University Press, 1957 (the definitive ed.).
    → HOLLIS: "Works of Jonathan Edwards Yale volume 1 Freedom of the Will"
  • Arminius / the Remonstrance — The Works of James Arminius, trans. James & William Nichols, Baker reprint (3 vols).
    → HOLLIS: "Works of James Arminius Nichols"
  • Barth, Epistle to the Romans — Karl Barth, trans. Edwyn C. Hoskyns, Oxford University Press, 1933 (from the 6th German ed.; many reprints). + election: Church Dogmatics II/2, ed. Bromiley & Torrance, T&T Clark.
    → HOLLIS: "Barth Epistle to the Romans Hoskyns" / "Barth Church Dogmatics II/2" ⚠ (Hoskyns may be copyright — we also hold 235 passages in the DB).
02Tier 1b · first library haul (modern, no free version)
  • Stendahl — easiest single checkout: Paul Among Jews and Gentiles, and Other Essays, Fortress Press, 1976 (contains the "Introspective Conscience" essay; the essay alone = Harvard Theological Review 56, 1963, 199–215).
    → HOLLIS: "Stendahl Paul Among Jews and Gentiles"
  • Bowens — Lisa M. Bowens, African American Readings of Paul: Reception, Resistance, and Transformation, Eerdmans, 2020 (one edition — clean).
    → HOLLIS: "Bowens African American Readings of Paul"
  • Cranfield (the elbow commentary) — C. E. B. Cranfield, A Critical and Exegetical Commentary on the Epistle to the Romans, International Critical Commentary (ICC), T&T Clark, 1975/79 (2 vols; 8:28–30 is in vol. 1).
    → HOLLIS: "Cranfield Romans ICC"
  • Lovejoy — Arthur O. Lovejoy, The Great Chain of Being: A Study of the History of an Idea, Harvard University Press, 1936 (the HUP paperback reprint is fine).
    → HOLLIS: "Lovejoy Great Chain of Being"

  • Dunn — James D. G. Dunn, Romans, Word Biblical Commentary 38A/38B, Word Books, 1988 (8:28–30 in 38A).

    → HOLLIS: "Kasemann Commentary on Romans Bromiley"

    → HOLLIS: "Moo Romans NICNT"
  • Wright — "Romans," in The New Interpreter's Bible vol. 10, Abingdon, 2002; + Paul and the Faithfulness of God, Fortress, 2013.
    → HOLLIS: "New Interpreter's Bible volume 10"
  • Sanders — E. P. Sanders, Paul and Palestinian Judaism, Fortress Press, 1977.
    → HOLLIS: "Sanders Paul and Palestinian Judaism"
  • Fitzmyer — Joseph A. Fitzmyer, Romans, Anchor Bible 33, Doubleday, 1993.

  • Weber — "The routinization of charisma," in Economy and Society, ed. Roth & Wittich, University of California Press, 1978.
    → HOLLIS: "Weber Economy and Society Roth Wittich"
  • Kuhn — The Structure of Scientific Revolutions, University of Chicago Press, 4th ed. 2012 (Hacking intro).
    → HOLLIS: "Kuhn Structure of Scientific Revolutions 4th"

    → HOLLIS: "Turner Ritual Process"
  • Cantwell Smith — The Meaning and End of Religion, Fortress Press, 1991 reprint (orig. Macmillan 1962).
    → HOLLIS: "Cantwell Smith Meaning and End of Religion"
  • Cone (✅ also in DB) — A Black Theology of Liberation, Orbis, 40th Anniversary ed. 2010; God of the Oppressed, Orbis, rev. 1997.
    → HOLLIS: "Cone Black Theology of Liberation Orbis"
  • Skinner — "Meaning and Understanding in the History of Ideas," History and Theory 8 (1969); reprinted in Visions of Politics, vol. 1: Regarding Method, Cambridge UP, 2002.
    → HOLLIS: "Skinner Visions of Politics volume 1"
  • Meadows — Thinking in Systems: A Primer, Chelsea Green, 2008.
    → HOLLIS: "Meadows Thinking in Systems"
  • Moretti — Distant Reading, Verso, 2013; Graphs, Maps, Trees, Verso, 2005.
    → HOLLIS: "Moretti Distant Reading"
  • James — The Varieties of Religious Experience — the Harvard University Press critical ed., 1985 (or any Penguin Classics).
    → HOLLIS: "James Varieties of Religious Experience Harvard"
  • Lakoff & Johnson — Metaphors We Live By, University of Chicago Press, 2003 ed.
    → HOLLIS: "Lakoff Johnson Metaphors We Live By"
