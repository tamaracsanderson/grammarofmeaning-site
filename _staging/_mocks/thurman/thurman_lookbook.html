Grammar of Meaning

Voice garden · Howard Thurman · look & feel

Five feels — before the skin

A lookbook, not a build. Every direction keeps the Grammar of Meaning spine — warm ground, Newsreader, hairline restraint — but each proposes what's new for this particular medium: a random, contemplative encounter with Thurman's own recorded voice. Pick a feel; we develop it from here.

The constant across all five: spin → decelerate → land on a real Thurman sermon → read the excerpt, hear the recording. What changes below is the feel, never the mechanic.

How to read this These are directions, not builds. Each is a feel we can develop into the real spinning experience — some are close cousins (01 and 04 both work in the dark; 03 and 05 both lean on the paper house), some are far apart. Pick one to take forward, or a pairing to hybridise (e.g. Field Notes structure + Resonance motion). Then — and only then — we build the skin. GoM spine kept throughout; Thurman-only; Stage 6 = 0 — sample titles are catalogued, sample lines are real Thurman phrases to be §13-verified before any publish.
