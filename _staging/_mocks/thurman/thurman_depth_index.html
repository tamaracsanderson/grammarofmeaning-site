Going deeper — the depth-layer & the companion-mirror

The garden's surface is beautiful — a fragment floats up and settles back. What's missing is depth (nowhere to sit and read a whole passage or see the art large) and the mirror (a quiet companion that reflects your path back). Seven divergent answers, from immersive to anti-immersive. Depth is always opt-in — the surface stays ephemeral.

Every companion honors all seven guardrails — no streak, no score, private, a mirror-not-a-face (it does not know you, and it is not Thurman), maieutic not oracle, both the draw and the friction, and it points you off the screen. Companions are static demos of the register; production is inline Claude Max SDK ($0, single-user).

The seven mocks

1The Descentmy pick — pair w/ 6immersive

Click and you sink full-screen down through the deep river into a still chamber; the passage sits in a shaft of light. A single maieutic line is summoned at the deep margin. Most faithful to Thurman's own image.
2The Drawercalm

A reading panel slides in from the right; the water surface stays visible behind. The companion is quiet marginalia in the drawer's outer edge. The least disruptive — closest to a conventional web pattern.
3The Talmud pagescholarly

The passage centered, hemmed by commentary. The companion isn't bolted on — it is the outer gloss, and it writes your path into the margin at close. Literal hevruta.
4The Gallery roombehold

You walk into a walled room; the art hangs large with its wall-label and a bench. A docent's card offers one question you can pick up. The installation register you liked, as the deep view.
5The Tidepoetic

Motion inverted — the fragment rises, the world recedes below the waterline. Ring a bell for a question; at close the tide goes out and leaves one thing on the sand. The most distinctive gesture; the riskiest to build well.
6The Slow-readmy pick — the calm flooranti-immersive

One line at a time, at your own pace; no water, no motion. The companion is deferred entirely to a single closing reflection that points you outward. The opposite of a feed — the "soil."
7The Cartographymirror-artifact

The mirror made the whole subject: the session's map — gravity wells, a pivot, drift — naming the draw and the friction, then pointing outward. This is what "reflect back" actually looks like as an object.

design-SB's recommendation

Build a spine of two, and steal one component. Ship #1 The Descent as the default depth-open — it's the most faithful (you literally descend into Thurman's deep river) and it holds the surface/depth distinction cleanly. Offer #6 The Slow-read as the calm alternate for text-only pieces (the "soil" — the floor everything else rests on). And fold #7 The Cartography in as the close of any session, regardless of which room you were in — it's the one that makes the Kohut mirror a real object rather than a chat transcript. #3 (Talmud) is the strongest if the audience is scholarly; #2/#4/#5 are excellent but each is a narrower bet (2 = safe, 4 = art-only, 5 = beautiful but the hardest motion to land without kitsch).
