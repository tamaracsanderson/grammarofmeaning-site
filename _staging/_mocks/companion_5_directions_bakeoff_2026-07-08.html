Five iterations of the GoM look

Same house language, same screen — the Romans 8:28–30 slow-read — five different modes of it.

Five outfits on one body, not five bodies. The GoM identity is fixed — the same brandmark (above) + favicon + house pattern, the same palette family (gom.css: paper · moss · fern · sage · olive · terracotta · gold), the two registers (illuminated Word / spare ink body), and the botanical system. What varies is design-mode · layout · UI · interaction — one leans slow-reading, one handmade-web, one Morris, one minimalism, one maximalism (green-forward, still in-palette). The lens-selector is the thing they actually differ on — a margin-tab · a hand-drawn note · a Morris menu · a spare dial · an illuminated index — because how the reader picks a lens is the companion's core UI. The slow-read screen is built so the three hero exhibits drop in (reception-diptych → the Sitz view · translation-morph → a Sitz-dial · golden-chain-hole → the held "why"). Pick one, or synthesize.

01

Slow reading

one line at a time, your pace — the calm reading-first mode mode · reveal & breath

What varies

Layout: a single narrow column, centered, huge line-height; almost no chrome. UI: the passage arrives one line at a time (the next lines wait, faint); a breathing pace-dot sets the tempo. Interaction: tap / scroll reveals the next line; the golden chain builds link-by-link as you read.

Palette weighting

paperinkfern accent

The lightest weighting — paper & ink, one fern thread. Air is the material.

Ghost / demonstrator

The withheld "why" arrives as its own faint line, then holds — never resolves. A bodily practice opens a quiet line-figure below the text.

02

Handmade web

the maker's hand shows — drawn rules, inked circles, gentle imperfection mode · craft & warmth

What varies

Layout: the same card, but every rule and underline is hand-drawn (wobbly SVG strokes), lightly off-axis. UI: chain-links are ink circles joined by a dashed pen-line; the highlight is a squiggle underline. Interaction: the chain inks itself link by link (draw-on), like a hand annotating.

Palette weighting

terracottaolivegold

Warm weighting — terracotta & olive lead; the hand-drawn edge keeps it human.

Ghost / demonstrator

The "why" is a pencilled aside in the maker's hand. The demonstrator lives on a separate bodily-practice screen — deferred (it needs a real hand-drawn figure, not an icon).

Reference

the_handmade_web sensibility · a made-by-a-person feel.

03

Morris

the reading inside a living pattern — Arts & Crafts botanical, restrained mode · pattern & frame

What varies

Layout: the text sits inside a botanical frame on a faint repeating leaf-and-berry pattern (GoM sage/olive/gold). UI: corner sprigs mark the frame; the chain-dots are small gilded berries. Interaction: the border grows (a vine unfurls) as you move through the four stages.

Palette weighting

sageolivemossgold

Botanical weighting — sage/olive pattern, moss text, gold berries. Ornament that stays quiet.

Ghost / demonstrator

The "why" is a gap in the pattern — a bare patch of vellum the vine leaves un-grown. Demonstrator sits among the leaves.

Reference

William Morris × the Botanic-Atlas / Audubon botanical plates × the corner-botanical system.

04

Minimalism

near-nothing — huge margins, a hairline chain, one accent mode · restraint

What varies

Layout: vast margins, a small measure; the chain is a single hairline with five ink points. UI: tiny mono labels; the Greek, the sub-head, the ornament — all removed. Interaction: everything hidden until touched; one line, one word, one gap surfaced at a time.

Palette weighting

paperinkone fern line

The barest weighting — paper, ink, a single fern underline. Swiss restraint in house clothes.

Ghost / demonstrator

The "why" is simply the space after the last link — unmarked, unfilled. No demonstrator on the reading screen; it appears only when a practice is chosen.

Reference

The house at its quietest · cand_editorial clarity.

05

Maximalism

a rich green ground — gilded chain, botanical corners, everything present mode · abundance (green ground)

What varies

Layout: a deep moss-green ground (still the house palette), gold-leaf chain, botanical corners, paper-cream text — the page as a rich garden at dusk. UI: the chain glows; embellished lens-chips; everything visible at once. Interaction: the gold links catch light as you dwell; corners bloom on entry (reduced-motion → still).

Palette weighting

moss groundgoldsageterracotta

The richest weighting — moss/fern green as the ground (your green-in-palette idea), gold + sage + terracotta embellishment on top.

Ghost / demonstrator

The "why" is a darker unlit region past the last gold link — present but unilluminated. The demonstrator lives on a separate bodily-practice screen — deferred (needs a real hand-drawn figure).

Reference

The Botanic-Atlas richness × Audubon plates × an illuminated garden, in GoM green.

Recommendation (design-SB). These are all one family now — the choice is temperament, not aesthetic. My read: Slow reading is the truest to the companion's telos (the piece is a reading practice); Morris and Maximalism are where the botanical embellishment (Botanic-Atlas / Audubon) can live; Handmade carries the "authored, made-by-a-person" story; Minimalism is the safest, most legible baseline. Strong synthesis to build next: Slow-reading structure as the spine, with a mode-dial (like the Thurman components' palette/pixel dials) that lets a reader shift the same screen from minimal → Morris → maximal-green.
