The Companion · visual bake-off · staging

Five directions for a reading companion

Same screen — the Romans 8:28–30 slow-read — drawn five ways, so they can sit side by side.

The companion is a reading-first interface that reads one passage slowly. It holds the generative gap where two readings meet and deposits nothing — it draws out the reader's meaning, it never hands one down. This is a divergent pass: five relatively-different looks, each a full feel + palette, all members of one family (a nod to the GoM base — vellum · sage · gold · ink), each varying within it. Re-made, not skinned; for adults, never twee. Pick one, or synthesize.

Guards travel through all five. · deposits nothing (§2.1) — never asserts a reading or "traditions agree"; it holds the gap. · no gamification of the sacred (no scores/streaks); reduced-motion fallback on every motion. · CARE — represent-don't-simulate closed practices; the drawn demonstrator is open practices only (lectio-posture, Zazen, Kinhin); closed rites are honored/named, never a step-by-step figure. · re-make, not copy (no cloning living artists / the engraving look). · the rhyme is the reader's association (§2.6), never asserted convergence — moves rhyme, telos diverges. · two registers legible everywhere: illuminated received-Word (the golden chain) + spare sumi/ink enacted-practice (the demonstrator).

Body · EB Garamond. Mock renders a Palatino/Iowan fallback.

Texture / material

Vellum tooth + faint gold wash; drop-caps in vermilion; a Morris botanical border rules the page; gilded roundels catch light (highlight-inset, never flat).

Negative-space ghost

A sinopia — the withheld "why God chose these" as a faint red-ochre underdrawing beneath the chain. Rubricate, don't black-out.

Sumi demonstrator

Lives in the margin — a spare ink seated form beside the gilded text; ornate word / spare body, both the maker's hand.

Touchstone

Book of Kells × William Morris × a reverent, non-kitsch re-make.

Ink & Void

Body · the same face, small & airy. Near-monochrome; type recedes.

Texture / material

Raw paper grain; one dry-brush stroke; a single carved vermilion seal is the only colour on the page.

Negative-space ghost

The emptiness itself. The withheld "why" is the vast blank half — one-corner composition (Ma Yuan); the gap needs no drawing because it is the field.

Sumi demonstrator

Central and large — the seated gesture carries the screen; the chain is a thin marginal line. Spare body over ornate word.

Touchstone

The Study

a working manuscript — the reader's own hand in the margins the scholarly-intimate pole

Palette

warm-cream efe7d3sepia-ink 5f5849muted-gold a6893fannotation-red a33a2c

Type pairing

Display · a bookish transitional serif (Georgia / Charter)

Body · the same; margins in a hand face + mono for references.

Texture / material

Ruled commonplace-book paper; a red margin-rule; sepia diagram; the reader's pencil underlines and marginal notes; warm, not clinical.

Negative-space ghost

A marginal query in the reader's own hand — "why these?" pencilled beside the sealed link; the gap is annotated, never answered.

Sumi demonstrator

A small sepia figure sketched in the margin like a study; quiet, drawn-in-passing. Quiet marginalia is the only social layer — never comments.

Touchstone

An annotated codex × the commonplace-book × warm paper.

Recommendation (design-SB). If the companion should feel like a sacred object, lead with Illuminated Book and borrow Ink & Void's restraint so the gold never tips to kitsch — the two registers (illuminated word / spare body) are already the companion's spec, so this pairing is the most on-brief. If it should feel like an extension of the live site, Living Garden is the safest continuity but the least new. My pick to push next: Illuminated × Ink&Void hybrid for the received-Word screens + Study's marginalia for the reader's own layer. Your call — pick one, rank them, or name a synthesis and I'll build the chosen direction out to a real interactive slow-read.
