Our language, lightly glossed as glass

The window you liked — a faint diamond-quarry whisper of stained glass — with the medallions we already developed in the slots, and our own leaves and sprigs as the border: curling in the two bottom corners and the two rounded top corners, a rose at the crown. No medieval tracery, no heavy color — just our line-language, warm, with a glass gloss.

The re-weight, GoM-quiet: forward = a medallion in full gilded color (as here); receded = the same medallion dropped to a pale line-only version on the quarry (like a lightly-drawn panel) — so a 1840 window keeps Judgment + Atonement full while the others thin to line, and 2020 reverses it. Emphasis by presence, not by darkness.
