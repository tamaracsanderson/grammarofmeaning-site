Where the weights sit — altar layouts

Three ways the eight soteriology icons could stand together on a Christian altar — the full reredos, the centered altar, and the mini diagram — each drawn schematically in the house language, moss linework on warm ground with a sparing devotional nod.

Concept 1 · Immersive

The full reredos — “the whole screen is an altar”

Use-case: an immersive landing, or a “pilgrimage” screen you step into. A tall retable fills the frame; a raised central aedicule holds the focal weight, flanked by symmetrical rows of arched niches.

Concept 2 · Card

The centered altar — “an altar in the center of the screen”

Use-case: a section header, or a focal card within a longer page. A contained mensa with a low retable behind it — the eight icons in a 4-over-4 row of small arched niches, a cross above, a candle each side, generous margin all around.

Concept 3 · Scalable thumbnail

The mini altar diagram — “scalable thumbnail / legend”

Use-case: a nav glyph, a data-viz key, a repeatable motif. A simple line altar-table with a stepped retable outline; the eight icons reduced to small marks in their niche positions. Shown at two sizes to prove it reads down to a thumbnail.

On these layouts

This is the Christian altar only — a reredos, a mensa, a gradine, arched niches, candlesticks, a frontal: real church vocabulary, drawn schematically in the house language rather than rendered photorealistically. Each other tradition later gets its own sacred architecture on the same warm ground; nothing here is a universal frame.

Schematic and provisional. Stage 6 stands at 0 coded passages — which weight sits at the center, how the eight are ordered, and how prominently each is drawn are illustrative placements, not coded findings. The empty niche is kept genuinely empty in every layout: the apophatic remainder the tradition holds open.
