What to notice

Byzantine feels the most sacred — the warm ground + gold-leaf halo + lapis mandorla read immediately as an icon panel; red is reserved for the robe/banner, so it carries emotional weight without shouting. This is the “altar” register at its richest.

Passion / blood is the most solemn and weighty — bone figures glowing off charcoal, the red genuinely reading as blood. But the dark ground swallows silhouette detail: the 40px background versions lose the most here, and across 7 icons the palette risks monotony (everything is dark + red + gold).

Illuminated manuscript gives the most range for 7 distinct icons — four working hues (vermilion, ultramarine, gold, green) mean each of the seven components can lean on a different accent while sharing the gold ground, and its brighter contrast is what survives shrinking to 40px best.

On salience: in all three, the foreground icon is redder + gilded (saturated robe, gold halo/mandorla), and the background icon cools + flattens to a near-monochrome silhouette — so red-vs-gold is doing the foreground/background work, not just size.
