the living altar · direction 1 of 3–5 · the stained-glass window

The seven as lights in a window — and emphasis is which panels glow

One tradition's answer to how does a person get saved?, set in a traceried window: the four operations in the main lancets, the agency dial (Grace ↔ Effort) across the sill, Judgment presiding in the rose above, the sealed niche kept dark at the foot. Nothing is static — light is salience. Re-weight the tradition and different lights blaze while others go dark.

Why this placement: it borrows real church space — the Last Judgment historically lives high on the tympanum/rose where the congregation faces the reckoning; light (rose window, gold ground) is the medieval sign of divine presence, so making light the salience-carrier is native to the form, not imposed. The two windows below are the same glass, lit two ways.

state A · forensic / evangelical

“Christ paid the penalty; the books are opened”

≈ 1840

Judgment blazes in the rose; Atonement is the one lit operation; Liberation, Union, Communion go dark; the dial is hard-left — grace alone.

state B · inclusive / mainline

“Set-free and gathered-in; the reckoning softens”

≈ 2020

Judgment falls to a back-glow; Liberation + Communion blaze; Union warms to mid; Atonement darkens; the dial slides to mid; the sealed niche grows present.

How to read it. Both windows are the same seven lights — only the lighting changed. lit = foregrounded (high salience) · dark = backgrounded (present but unlit) · mid = warming/receding. The rose oculus is Judgment (the presiding horizon, not a peer-operation); the four lancets are the operations; the sill dial is the agency axis (Grace ↔ Effort); the foot quatrefoil is the sealed niche. Slide any of these and you have a new tradition's window. Schematic — Stage 6 = 0; the weights shown are illustrative, not coded findings.
