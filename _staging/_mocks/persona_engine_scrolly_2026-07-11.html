01

The text — what it says

She Loves You · SAID

A text is what it positively says. Here a friend is telling YOU about a woman you hurt — eight told beats (paraphrase).

M1 You hurt her.
M2 She suffered badly.
M3 She reconsidered — judges you’re not cruel.
M4 ★ core She loves you.
M5 You wrongly believe her love is gone.
M6 The friend saw her, and carries her word (the witness).
M7 So be glad.
M8 Swallow your pride and apologize.

Across all eight, the text speaks of her states, your actions, and the friend’s warrant — never once of your desire or love for her.

03

The detection grid

visibility × salience

Not every silence matters. The 2×2 — visibility (transparent ↔ opaque) × salience (out-of-focus ↔ in-focus) — says where to look.

← in-focus  ·  salience  ·  out-of-focus →
transparent · in-focus
“she loves you” — said, and it’s the point
opaque · in-focus
does he love her back? — felt, unresolved
transparent · out-of-focus
the friend’s errand — said, minor
opaque · out-of-focus
the weather that day — absent, irrelevant
transparent ← visibility → opaque

The seer — opaque to whom? A silence is opaque relative to a viewpoint: the agent (the addressee) may know his own heart; the narrator (the friend) and the reader cannot see it. The reciprocity is opaque to narrator + reader; the agent may know.

04

The money spot

opaque × in-focus

One cell is the money cell — opaque × in-focus — the gap the text makes you feel but won’t resolve. That’s where meaning is being withheld.

the money spot · M4 reciprocity

The form never states, implies, or answers it — the residual all six coders converged on (IRR177 + the SDK cold-coder).
05

That spot IS a gap — three things to keep apart

Decision 159

The gap isn’t one thing. It’s a present value, an absent-but-askable value, and the competing values that could stand in. Three different things — three colours.

Said
“she loves you”
the value present in the slot — lives in the CORE-7 grammar.
Unsaid
“does he love her back?”
absent but in-frame, detected + flavored — lives in the grid + flavors.
Alternative
“he doesn’t” / “he’s unsure”
a competing value that could have filled the slot — lives in the generation input.
06

The door — can it close?

M4 · foreclosed

Some gaps can be completed, some can only be replaced, some are barred until a later reader opens them.

Fill
…AND X — adds a value, stays in frame, keeps the said.
M4 · a FILL is proposable from a frame
Substitute
…X INSTEAD — retracts the said, jumps frame (that’s a fork, not a fill).
Barred
can’t even propose X — the move forecloses it.

M4 is foreclosed to the narrator — the friend can’t see the addressee’s heart — but the agent may know, so a FILL is proposable from a frame.

The frame-roles — who fills it?
