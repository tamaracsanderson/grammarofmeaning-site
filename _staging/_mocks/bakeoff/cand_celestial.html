01 — Palette

Luminous on indigo — and legible on daylight

Two grounds, one accent family. The deep ground is a true indigo (#16224A) — clearly a color, not a stand-in for black — so gold and ice can actually glow. On light, the same accents drop to ink-weights that pass contrast. No muddy greys anywhere; the neutrals are tinted blue.

Deep ground#16224A

True deep indigo. WCAG 15.1:1 vs gold — accents read as light, not smudge.

Raised indigo#1E2C55

Cards / panels on deep. One clear step up from ground — depth without grey.

Gold — glow accent#F5C451

Primary. Operations, kickers, active nav. Glows on deep; drops to #A5761A ink on day.

Ice — glow accent#7BE3FF

Secondary. Rhyme-edges, links, coordinates. Ink form #0A6D90 on day.

Rose — third light#EF8FB0

A different rhyme-class / outcome nodes. Used sparingly. Ink #B8447A on day.

Day ground#F4F1FF

A noon sky, not parchment. Blue-lavender white; ink is deep-indigo #1A2247 (14.8:1).

Ink / dim on deep#EEF1FF · #AAB3DD

Body + secondary text on deep. Tinted blue — never neutral grey.

A refined serif for prose, a precise sans for coordinates

The reading voice is warm and optical; the instrument voice is exact. A mono handles the star-coordinates and category codes.

Fraunces (display + prose) carries the humane, essayistic warmth. Space Grotesk (labels, nav, UI) is geometric and precise — the "instrument panel" voice. IBM Plex Mono handles coordinates, node IDs and category codes so the star-map reads like a real catalog.

03 — Texture & motif

The constellation is the structural language

CSS-only celestial: layered radial gradients for the well, scattered pin-stars, and drawn constellation lines. The motif everywhere is node + edge — a move is a star; a rhyme is the line between two stars in different constellations. No raster, no grey; the "texture" is light on indigo.

07 — "For the curious"

Secondary sections, designed — not dumped

The expandable "for the curious" is a first-class object: its own glyph, its own coordinate label, mini-fields inside. It rewards opening.

✦
Why call it a "move"?
for the curious · method
 ▸
A move is deliberately smaller than a "belief" and larger than a word. It is one operation with its arguments — the smallest unit that can rhyme across traditions without importing one tradition's theology into another. We read each source in its own vocabulary first (Greek proorizō, "to mark out beforehand"), then look for the shape underneath.
