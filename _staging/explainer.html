← Grammar of Meaning

🌸 The Garden — the instrument, in one view

A systems-thinking map of how meaning gets made across ~400 traditions of religion, philosophy, and psychology — not the doctrines they claim, but the actual operations, mined bottom-up from a corpus built for the purpose. This page is the project's orientation doc made parallel + zoomable: every section below expands; nothing here adds content beyond the doc.
The organizing idea is the metaxy — Plato's and Voegelin's "in-between." Meaning isn't made in raw experience, nor in finished doctrine; it's made in the between. Spirituality is the quality of attention that makes the in-between visible. The project maps what becomes visible there, at scale.

Mapping #1 — how a single observation morphs through institutional time T1–T10

An observation begins as lived experience — usually paradoxical, even paralyzing. To survive and scale it must be encoded, systematized, institutionalized. Each step preserves something and loses something. The praxis becomes teachable; the paradox often dissolves. The arc is tracked across ten stations by planting a seed source in each sub-tradition (Plato's Cave for philosophy, Romans 8:28–30 for Christianity, the Four Noble Truths for Buddhism…) and tracing how the seed morphs. Click any rung for the Cave worked example.
T1
Originating insight the return — alive
click for the Cave ⌄
A shattering realization: what you thought was real is illusion. The waking-up is violent and disorienting. And once you see, you're obligated to go back and free the others still trapped.
T2
Direct-witness teaching / first encoding
click ⌄
The insight becomes communicable — taught in speech, first put into words (T1 itself is asymptotic; only inferred). Socratic teaching as the Cave's first encoding.
T3
Textual codification
click ⌄
The teaching becomes fixed text — Republic Book VII. Canon-formation begins.
T4
Institutional canonization
click ⌄
An institution collects + canonizes the text (a collection-event): the Academy's curriculum; the philosophical canon.
T5
Scholarly commentary
click ⌄
Scholars argue + elaborate the doctrine, from inside (emic) or outside (etic) — the commentary tradition on the Republic (Proclus and after).
T6
Confessional / sub-tradition codification
click ⌄
Doctrine LOCKS as institutionally-binding subscription; sub-traditions diverge — Platonic body/soul dualism worn as a school badge.
T7
Degraded popular form — within the lineage the return
click ⌄
The codified form degrades into a devalued popular form INSIDE the lineage: "we're all just trapped in our own caves," the dorm-poster Cave — the violent waking-up and the obligation to return are gone.
T8
Revival / recovery movement the return — re-ignited
click ⌄
A recovery movement diagnoses the calcification and re-ignites the originating T1 — re-reading the ascent (and the return) as lived practice, against the slogan.
T9
Living lineage
click ⌄
Recovery normalizes into living, present-day continuation (~2 generations) — the Cave taught, argued, and practiced as live philosophy now.
T10
Artistic reimagination — outside the lineage · ORTHOGONAL, not a rung after T9 the return — re-staged
click ⌄
The Matrix (1999) — art re-encountering the mechanism in a wholly different epistemic mode. We mistake the simulation for the real, the awakening is violent, and Neo must choose to return and free others. Not degradation: a genuine re-staging in a new medium.
The casualty is specific. Degradation isn't generic fading — the "return to the cave" (going back to free the others) is exactly what the degraded popular form (T7) evacuates; revival (T8) re-ignites it inside the lineage, and T10 — orthogonal, art's own axis — re-stages it outside: The Matrix is a near-perfect re-encounter, the violent waking-up and the return. The ladder's color tells the same story: saturation drains toward T7 and returns at T8–T9. Stage names per the canonical T-stage lock (S99 + IRR97, all 10 locked) — full instrument page: scholars · history · the actual prompt. The Cave-specific placements at T2/T4–T8 are re-glossed candidates per §2.1 — researcher to confirm.
Zoom in: how the validated seeds generalize this
Once the pattern holds across the canonical seeds (Cave, Romans 8:28–30, Four Noble Truths, …), the plan expands to secondary seeds and traces multiple branching paths — the full space of variations, not one canonical arc. In the data this becomes the threads-tree: every passage anchored to a seed, assembled by its T-stage into the branching tree of the observation's institutional life (one branch into commentary T5, one into confessional lock T6, one into the degraded popular form T7, one into revival T8 and the living lineage T9 — with T10, artistic reimagination, as a parallel axis, not a rung). Mapping #2 — how experience becomes meaning Substrate → Operations → Outcomes → Paradigm

Most comparative work codes the static content of traditions (attributes, doctrines) or hunts a shared mystical essence. This project codes the actual moves by which experience becomes meaningful: raw experience gets structured by a grammar — the patterned moves that organize it into coherence — which reshapes a paradigm (its worldview). The grammar has three layers; the paradigm sits above them.
Place no-self in the stack:
Substrate no-self
what a tradition attends to — restlessness of mind; the fact of loss; here: a felt, fixed self
The substrate no-self acts on: the felt, fixed self.
Operations no-self
how it acts — practices (silence, ritual) and construals (re-seeing the self as empty, time as cyclical)
As an operation, no-self is a move — loosening the self-construct (what a Zen sūtra teaches you to do).
Outcomes no-self
what emerges — states (equanimity), ethics (care that widens), symbolic orders, restructured identities
As an outcome, no-self is a product — a less rigid, restructured identity.
Paradigm no-self
the worldview the grammar reshapes — claims, held little-t or Big-T
As a paradigm, no-self is a claim — held little-t ("the self is fluid, plural") or Big-T ("there is, finally, no self").
Where a thing sits decides what it does — that's the grammar (like a word's role in a sentence). The same content can be a move, a product, or a claim. And the moves recur across traditions: they rhyme, they don't match — which is mapping #3.

Mapping #3 — how meanings rhyme across traditions A : B :: C : D

Comparative religion usually splits: traditions share a universal essence (perennialism), or they're utterly particular (no comparison possible). Relational rhyme escapes the binary. Instead of hand-picking comparisons, a cross-tradition analogy graph mines the database for relational structures that recur — scored and searchable — so you see both the convergence and the difference.
fasting : appetite :: silence : speech
Both are disciplined restraint of a faculty — yet fasting and silence look nothing alike. The relation rhymes; the terms don't match. That's how structure surfaces even across traditions with opposed worldviews.

What this isn't. No claim the traditions knew they rhymed; no metaphysical proof. A rhyme is a structural observation — and rhyme across traditions is precisely what makes it interesting (each rhyme-pair carries a contact-spectrum flag). The graph is built to surface these at scale; it hasn't yet — 13 confirmed so far, mined from the first 16 flowers.
Zoom in: a rhyme is an edge
In the data structure, mapping #2's coding gives every passage its layer-coordinates (substrate / operation / outcome / paradigm). A rhyme is a horizontal edge between operations in different traditions whose relational structure matches (A:B::C:D) — zero lexical overlap required. The similarity is computed on the operation (the verb-test), not on words. That's the originality claim: not a bag-of-words frequency study.

How it's built — the pipeline Acquire → Chunk → Code → Find

Everything above runs on one factory line — raw archives in, cross-tradition patterns out — at three stages of completion: the corpus is built, the coding is live, the pattern-finding is designed and downstream. Below: the four phases, then the full 14-station value-stream with two worked examples — TENGRI ("we read Wolf Totem; we need Mongolian sky-worship" — acquisition-heavy, shows the front of the line) and ROMANS (the matured flower — analysis-heavy, shows the back).

Find

Per-passage tags assemble into two structures: threads (the T1–T10 branching tree per seed) and rhyme (the cross-tradition analogy graph).

What each passage gets — the coding-card the current frontier

Every passage is coded first in its tradition's own vocabulary, then mapped onto the axes below. Shown as one card, for the Romans seed:
Romans 8:28 — "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God…" ABR-CHR · Pauline
Docentorientation
2–3 sentences: what is this practice or claim for? What problem does it solve? How would an outsider recognize it?
Grammar → Paradigmmapping #2
Is this a practice/move (operation) or a doctrinal claim (interpretation)? Code both if both appear.
Lifecycle T1–T10mapping #1
Where on the lineage chain: originating insight (T1) → first encoding (T2) → textual codification (T3) → canonization (T4) → scholarly commentary (T5) → confessional lock (T6) → degraded popular form (T7) → revival (T8) → living lineage (T9) — plus T10 (artistic reimagination), orthogonal.
Scale S0–S8coordinate
The widest circle of concern the passage values: S0 parts-of-self · S1 individual · S2 dyad · S3 face-to-face group · S4 institution · S5 society · S6 tradition-across-generations · S7 multispecies/ecological · S8 cosmos.
Depthcoordinate
How deep and practiced is the attention to the in-between itself? Low = theory only · Medium = named practice with method · High = enacted experience, and the tradition knows its own failure modes. Scale and Depth are independent: a wide circle can be shallow; a narrow circle can be deep.
Provenanceaudit
Source origin, translation, artifact type, harvest method — every coded value is verifiable back to its row.
Insiderfluent in the tradition's primary sources
Outsiderfluent in comparative categories
Practitionerlives the tradition
Three independent reviewers; consensus never forced. When all three agree — convergent data. When they diverge — the disagreement is data: flag it (translation? scope? genuine difference?). Reliability is quantified per cell (Krippendorff-α-style agreement), so the answer to "quant or qual?" is: qual-grounded, quant-validated — interpretation under the grammar, measured by inter-rater agreement and convergence counts. Not a word-frequency study.
Zoom in: "isn't that too many codes?"
Six fields, and each is load-bearing for exactly one job: Docent feeds the interface (orientation for a visitor); Grammar→Paradigm is mapping #2's layer assignment; T-stage is mapping #1's ladder position; Scale + Depth are the two independent coordinates that make the SCALE×DEPTH plane navigable (and the widening-circle rhymes detectable); Provenance is the audit trail. Remove any one and a mapping loses its data. The discipline that keeps it honest: the first coding cycle uses only the tradition's own vocabulary — the axes are applied on top, never instead.

The interface — three structures, navigable in space + the garden

Coding makes three structures real; the Archive makes them navigable. The interaction model is semantic zoom (the dynamic-abstractions move): the same structure at different altitudes —
🔭 garden (all flowers) → 🌳 grove (a tradition) → 🌸 flower (a seed's thread) → 🍃 passage (one coded atom) — zoom out again and the rhyme-edges light up between groves.

The threads-tree

per seed: every anchored passage assembled by T-stage into the branching tree of institutional time — one branch into commentary, one into the degraded form, one into revival and the living lineage; T10 runs orthogonal.

The rhyme-graph

cross-tradition: A:B::C:D analogy edges between operations in different groves — scored, searchable, contact-flagged.

The SCALE × DEPTH plane

every passage placed by its two independent coordinates — wide-but-shallow and narrow-but-deep become visibly different regions.

How to read the garden (the live render: data/chunk_quality/_garden/garden.html)

🌱seed — the planted source (Romans 8:28–30; the Cave; the Four Noble Truths): one per sub-tradition.
🌸flower — a seed in bloom: its coded passages, threaded through institutional time.
🎋stem / thread — the vertical axis: T1→T10 within a tradition. The branching is the finding.
🌳grove — a tradition; its own palette (the per-tradition flare — divergence kept visible).
↔️rhyme-edge — the horizontal axis: the same move appearing across groves, contact-flagged.
🎼concordance — the shared grammar all groves draw on. Concordance + flares together = the convergence question, made visual.
The two axes are the thesis question: the stem asks "how does one observation morph within a tradition?" (Q1); the rhyme-edge asks "which moves rhyme across traditions?" (Q2). The garden is the experiential layer of the same instrument — and the eventual Garden is this garden as pilgrimage.

Two outputs

The coding is the instrument; two things are built on top of it:
✍️ Essays — Grammar of Meaning
Field notes on how meaning gets made. Each begins from a seed (a passage, a word, a pattern); a drafting pipeline renders five forms, runs a review panel, polishes against a voice guide; the researcher hand-edits the v1. The same multi-voice-panel method as the coding, pointed at prose — AI drafts the breadth; she stays the author. grammarofmeaning.org, Substack as teaser. The essay engine's own pipeline page →
🏛 The Garden
An interactive pilgrimage: a digital constellation of traditions' observations where the exploring itself is the formation. The Docent voice orients at each seed, so someone can meet a tradition they didn't grow up in.
Nothing in the machinery is religion-specific — it's a domain-agnostic instrument for mapping how a field makes sense of its object. Religion is the proof-of-concept. The corpus is living: it grows and chunks itself as its reach widens.

The three technical frontiers where input lands

⭐ Semantic indexing & retrieval-fairness — the frontier known least, needed most
The corpus is wide on purpose (the bias-guard: you can only find what's in it) — but only ~0.15% is semantically indexed, so retrieval is still keyword-based, and keyword search structurally starves the overlooked voices (a womanist reading of Romans doesn't keyword-match "Romans 8:28"). Three open questions: (1) which embedding model for a multilingual theological corpus (Greek, Sanskrit, Arabic, Hebrew, Japanese reception) without burying the non-English voices; (2) diversity-aware retrieval — don't let nearest-neighbour return more of the dominant voice (84% Luther on Genesis); (3) how to evaluate fairness with no ground-truth labels.
Database & coding infrastructure at scale
~390k sources / ~9.5M passages, coding live, mid-migration SQLite→Postgres/pgvector. Queryability at scale; scaling constraints; validating multi-LLM coding reliability across the corpus (first answer in: the cheap model matches the expensive 3-model consensus 100% on T-staging).
The Archive interface + the rhyme embedding
The three structures above, navigable in one space. The open core: what embedding surfaces structural / operational proximity (proximity = rhyme — the "same move"; distinct from the topical retrieval index)? How to render time + branching together? One navigable space or separate views?
The breadth goal that frames all three: ~100 flowers — multiple seeds per tradition and across sub-traditions, weighted toward the lenses the dominant voices drown out (womanist, liberation, disability, queer). Not volume-chasing: a rhyme that holds across Luther and a womanist reading is far stronger than one that only holds in the comfortable mainstream — and rhymes scale pairwise, so more nodes means combinatorially more genuine rhymes. The coding stays bounded: the curated set (~10–20K nodes), not the 9.5M corpus.
