An illuminated page — with the holes left open

A scribe would gild the words and leave the losses blank. Here we do the reverse: the golden chain of Romans 8 is illuminated, and the three places the text falls silent are cut open as lacunae — voids in the page. Around them, two readers write their glosses; a third reads the spectrum between. We measure the in-between; we don't fill it. Augustine of Hippo
354–430 · bishop of Hippo Regius, Roman North Africa · the anti-Pelagian; salvation by unmerited grace
◀ the predestinarian pole

On the basis of election — sheer grace / God's bare purpose — not works.

On reprobation — affirmed: the non-foreknown perish.

On agency — absorbed into God's purpose (perseverance itself a gift).

The Golden Chain
foreknew · predestined · called · justified · glorified

Three gilt verbs above the first lacuna, two below the last — the chain runs unbroken in the text; the holes are what it doesn't say.

The Arminian tradition
after Jacobus Arminius, 1560–1609 · Leiden · the Remonstrance (1610); conditional election, resistible grace
the synergist pole ▶

On the basis of election — foreknowledge of a foreseen response, made prior to predestination.

On reprobation — denied, by reductio ("the horrible decree").

On agency — free agency affirmed as independent, self-evident.

Thomas Aquinas · the mediating reader · the depth

2 · THE HOLE
Three lacunae

The margins argue
