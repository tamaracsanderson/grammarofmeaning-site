The method at work · reception discrimination

One verse. Three holes. Watch the map sort the readers.

The negative-space instrument reads a text for its holes — the questions the words leave open — then sorts the theologies that rush in to fill them. This is instrument-validation, not a theological verdict: coding has barely begun (Stage 6 = 0 coded passages), so what you see below is a candidate — a worked demonstration, not a finding. We measure the in-between; we don't fill it.

Explore it yourself. The three holes are hidden in the verse below. Click a marker — or a card — to open that hole to its two readers, and drill in to earn the third.

Overview — the verse and its golden chain
Romans 8:28–30 · KJV And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God… For whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son… Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.
foreknew→ predestined→ called→ justified→ glorified Five verbs — God is the subject of every one. The human is only ever the object. That grammar is where the holes open.

↓ Three holes to open. Pick one.

Hole B2 · basis of election

The chain begins at “foreknew” — but never says on what ground God foreknew.

Augustine Augustine of Hippo — 354–430 CE · bishop of Hippo Regius, Roman North Africa (modern Algeria) · led the anti-Pelagian controversy; taught salvation by unmerited grace, original sin, predestination (Confessions, City of God). The predestinarian pole.
Predestinarian pole
sheer grace / God's bare purpose — not works
Arminian The Arminian tradition — after Jacobus Arminius, 1560–1609 · Leiden, Dutch Republic · his followers issued the Remonstrance (1610). Taught conditional election (God elects those he foreknows will believe), resistible grace, free human response. The synergist pole.
Synergist pole
foreknowledge of a foreseen response, made prior to predestination
