First, the readers

Three people read this same chain — and disagreed about what it leaves out.

Across fifteen centuries, three readers pressed on the same five verbs. None disputed the words. They disputed the silences.

The predestinarian pole
Augustine of Hippo
354–430 CE · bishop of Hippo Regius, Roman North Africa (modern Algeria)

Led the anti-Pelagian controversy; taught salvation by unmerited grace, original sin, predestination (Confessions, City of God).

The synergist pole
The Arminian tradition
after Jacobus Arminius, 1560–1609 · Leiden, Dutch Republic

His followers issued the Remonstrance (1610): conditional election (God elects those he foreknows will believe), resistible grace, free human response.

The mediating reader
Thomas Aquinas
1225–1274 · Dominican friar, Paris & Naples

The Summa Theologiae (I, q.23): God's will the ground; reprobation by permission; double causality — God first cause, free will a real secondary cause.

Hole B2 — basis of election

The chain begins — but on what ground?

"On what ground was anyone foreknown?" The chain starts at foreknew, yet never says why God foreknew whom he foreknew.

Look left: the gap sits at the very first link, foreknew. The word is there; its reason is missing. That missing reason is the hole.
Augustine fills it
sheer grace / God's bare purpose — not works
The Arminian fills it
foreknowledge of a foreseen response, made prior to predestination
Aquinas, between them
God's goodness resolving into sheer divine will; rejects foreseen-merits

Near-binary. Augustine and Aquinas cluster tight on one end; the Arminian sits far off. On this hole, the dispute is almost categorical — two camps, not a gradient.

Hole B1 — reprobation

And those not on the chain?

"What of those not chosen?" The chain names only the saved — foreknown, called, justified, glorified. It says nothing about those left off it.

Look left: every link names the foreknown. The hole is everyone the chain doesn't mention — the silence around the saved.
Augustine fills it
affirmed — the non-foreknown perish
The Arminian fills it
denied, by reductio ("the horrible decree")
Aquinas, between them
affirmed but permission-not-causation ("endured" = "permitted"; gift-not-debt)

A genuine spectrum. Aquinas sits squarely in the middle — reprobation affirmed, but as permission, not cause. Here the dispute is a gradient, not two camps.

Hole B3 — where agency enters

Where does the human act?

Every verb is God's — foreknew, predestined, called, justified, glorified. The human is only ever the object. So where does the human's own agency enter?

Look left: God is the subject of all five verbs. The hole is the missing human subject — the place where a person would act, if they act at all.
Augustine fills it
absorbed into God's purpose (perseverance itself a gift)
The Arminian fills it
free agency affirmed as independent, self-evident
Aquinas, between them
double-causality — free will a real secondary cause, contained under God as first cause

A genuine spectrum again. Double-causality places Aquinas mid-axis — free will real, but held under God as first cause. A gradient, not a binary.

The payoff

The map sorted the theologies — and the shape of each dispute.

Three holes in one verse. Three readers. The instrument located each gap in the actual text, then sorted where each reader stands — and read the shape of every dispute: one hole nearly categorical (B2 — two camps), two genuinely gradient (B1, B3 — a spectrum with Aquinas in the middle).

Sorted, not scored. The instrument discriminates between readings; it never decides which is right.

How to read this — method integrity
  1. The move We read the verse for its negative space — the things a text leaves unsaid, not the things it states.
  2. The hole Each gap (B2 ground · B1 reprobation · B3 agency) is located in the actual verse, not imposed from outside.
  3. Who fills it Augustine, Aquinas, and the Arminian each fill the same holes differently — and the instrument sorts them, without ranking.
  4. Why this is instrument-validation This shows the instrument works — it finds real holes and sorts real readers. It is not a theological finding: the coding has barely begun (candidate, not conclusion). We measure the in-between; we don't fill it.
