Grammar of Meaning · The Method at Work

One text. Three holes. The map sorts who fills them.

Paul's chain of five verbs runs foreknew → predestined → called → justified → glorified — God the actor of every one. The negative-space instrument reads what the chain leaves unsaid, then sorts how later readers fill each silence — and even the shape of the disagreement.

This is method integrity, not apology: we measure the in-between; we don't fill it. The fills below are documented reception, a candidate demonstration of the instrument — not a coded finding.

The text, and the gaps inside it

Romans 8:28–30 (KJV) — “And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God… For whom he did foreknowB2 · on what ground?, he also did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son… Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also calledB3 · where does the human act?: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.”B1 · and those not chosen?

Five gold-underlined verbs = the golden chain. Three ◦ marks = the negative space — the questions the chain raises but never answers.

Augustine · 354–430, Hippo — the predestinarian pole Aquinas · 1225–74, Paris — the mediating reader Arminian · after Arminius, 1560–1609 — the synergist pole
B2

Basis of election

The chain begins at “foreknew” — but never says on what ground God foreknew.

← grace / God's bare willforeseen response →

Near-binary. Augustine and Aquinas cluster tight at one pole; the Arminian sits far off. On this hole the readers barely gradate.

Augustine

sheer grace / God's bare purpose — not works

Arminian

foreknowledge of a foreseen response, made prior to predestination

Aquinas · mediating

God's goodness resolving into sheer divine will; rejects foreseen-merits

B1

Reprobation

The chain names only the saved. What of those not foreknown?

← affirmed, by causedenied →

A genuine spectrum. Aquinas sits squarely in the middle — the readers spread evenly across the axis, not into two camps.

Augustine

affirmed — the non-foreknown perish

Arminian

denied, by reductio (“the horrible decree”)

Aquinas · mediating

affirmed but permission-not-causation (“endured” = “permitted”; gift-not-debt)

B3

Where agency enters

Every verb is God's; the human is only the object. Where does the human act?

← absorbed into Godindependent free agency →

A genuine spectrum. Again Aquinas holds the middle — free will a real secondary cause, neither pole.

Augustine

absorbed into God's purpose (perseverance itself a gift)

Arminian

free agency affirmed as independent, self-evident

Aquinas · mediating

double-causality — free will a real secondary cause, contained under God as first cause

How to read this — the instrument in four steps
1 · THE MOVE

Paul chains five verbs with God as sole actor.

2 · THE HOLE

The chain leaves gaps — ground, the unchosen, agency.

3 · WHO FILLS IT

Later readers fill each gap differently — sorted, not scored.

4 · WHY IT MATTERS

The map discriminates fills — a candidate demonstration; nothing here is a coded finding.
