A contemporary text serif with an optical-size axis — warm and human at reading sizes, then sharpened into a confident display face for headlines. This is the voice of the Essays and the reading pages: literary, but modern, never antiquarian.

Inter carries every label, nav item, and diagram annotation at high legibility — a neutral, exacting workhorse. IBM Plex Mono handles data, citations, figure numbers, and the reading-time chip, giving the research an instrument-panel precision.

Pairing rationale. A warm literary serif for meaning (Newsreader) against an exacting neo-grotesk for structure (Inter), arbitrated by a mono for data (Plex Mono). The serif says human; the grotesk says serious contemporary publication; the mono says this is measured, not asserted. Three registers, one clear hierarchy — and one skin over all three.

04Convergence · The Rhyme
E · Rhyme card — the same shape, spoken in two voices
Cross-tradition · candidate rhyme

The same shape, spoken in two voices.

Two poems rhyme when their endings agree, even though the words differ. A cross-tradition rhyme is the same: two moves share a shape — agent, substrate, operation, outcome — while the vocabulary, the metaphysics, and the world behind them stay entirely their own. Pauline "conformed to the image" and a Confucian account of becoming a junzi make the same move — a self reshaped toward a pattern held in advance — while disagreeing on almost everything else.

Not equivalence
We never flatten one tradition into another. The Big-T claims stay tradition-internal.
Not influence
Two voices can land on one shape on their own. Whether the two ever touched is a separate question.
Portable, little-t
The recurring move travels. That portability is what the study is after.
Earned, not assumed
A candidate rhyme stays "unranked" until the corpus supports it.

The workings, unhurried

Open only what draws you.
For the curious The full anatomy of a move — all seven parts 3 min

The public card shows four beats — agent, substrate, operation, outcome — because that is enough to read a move at a glance. But a fully coded move carries seven components. Here is the whole specimen, unhurried.

  • 1Operationthe verb — the single move being made (e.g. predestined).
  • 2Substratethe material worked on — who or what the operation acts upon.
  • 3Outcomethe result produced — what the substrate becomes.
  • 4Agencywho acts — the agent, and whether the move is done, received, or shared.
  • 5Warranton what authority — the ground the tradition gives for the move.
  • 6Consequencewhat follows — the stakes, the downstream of the move landing.
  • 7Scalehow far it reaches — one soul, a people, a cosmos.

Field note. The four public beats are a reading aid, not the model. Coding always records all seven; the branch diagram simply hides the last three until you open the drawer — so the glance stays clean and the depth stays available.
