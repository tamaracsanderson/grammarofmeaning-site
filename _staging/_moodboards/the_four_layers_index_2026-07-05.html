The garden's four layers 🌿

reading · ritual · interactivity · treatment — the map, the homes, and the thing that makes them cohere: they compose. (reading-SB, S138 · 2026-07-05)

Two layers are CONTENT (what comes from the traditions); two are FORM (the digital rendering). Content answers what a person does with a text/practice; form answers how the garden delivers it.

🎨 4 · TreatmentFORMthe art / aesthetic rendering — how a piece LOOKSthe treatment roster / aesthetic direction

The chat = layer 3's flagship for the companion surface: the dialogic reading-companion (read with a conversational partner). One interactivity pattern among many.

★ Why they're "different but interrelated": they COMPOSE

The layers aren't competing lists — they're the stack of any single garden encounter. One encounter = a content-practice (reading OR ritual) × an interactivity mechanic × a treatment, placed on a surface (library / garden / companion / essays — the WHERE).

Romans 8, as a walk = lectio (📖 reading) × the journey-journal (✋ interactivity) × shān-shuǐ ink-landscape (🎨 treatment), on the companion. Add an ambient bell pause (🧘 ritual) → all four layers in one encounter.
The negative-space poem = the artifact (content) × Meanders winding-path (✋/🎨) on the garden/essays. The three treatments (koan / blackout / meanders) are layer-4 variants of one piece.
The I Ching hexagram-encounter = a bibliomancy practice (🧘 ritual) × the cast-and-reveal (✋ interactivity) × a quiet card treatment (🎨), on the garden.

The axes are orthogonal: the same journey-journal mechanic could be rendered in shān-shuǐ OR hand-drawn graph-paper (treatment varies, mechanic constant); the same shān-shuǐ treatment could dress a reading OR a ritual. That is why four lists, not one — and why they interrelate: you pick one from each to make an encounter.
