"She Loves You" — the bidirectional trace-tree

The same instrument that regresses a church formula back to the New Testament, run on a secular pop song. Backward to its roots, forward to its reception, joined at the song as pivot.

Why pair the secular with the sacred. The golden-chain tree traces a sacred formula: doctrine ←backward← NT source →forward→ reception. This tree is its structural twin on a 1963 pop single — same method, different corpus. If the identical machinery produces a legible tree on "She Loves You," the method is shown to be about the grammar of transmission, not about scripture. That transferability is the claim the pairing is meant to test.
Every node and every edge below is a CANDIDATE, not a finding (§2.1). Not written to the DB. Stage 6 = 0.

Backward — the provenance roots (the un-run direction)

Five candidate roots the song's moves regress toward. This direction has failure modes the forward run never tested — teleological bias is maximal, and provenance hallucination is genre-specific. Handle every one of these as a hypothesis.

Three documented reception nodes — a mirror of the golden-chain forward fan. And the two residual gaps the song cannot fill from inside (per the 5/5-cold IRR177 correction): where reception must step in.

Every reconstructed link + every judgment call, surfaced for tomorrow. Dispose each: LOCK / EXTEND / DROP / DEFER.

  1. [RECONSTRUCTED EDGE] R2 third-person love-report → M4/M5. The form ("she loves you," a report about a third party rather than first-person "I love you") is textual — it's in the title.
