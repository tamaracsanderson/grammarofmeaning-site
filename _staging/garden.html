The rhymes (13) — sorted by adjudication tier

Read with a caveat (S109 essay-SB dogfood): most rhymes here share some form of "doctrinal codification" — but all current flowers are canonical foundational texts coded mostly at their T2/T5 (codification) stages, so "they all codify a teaching" is close to a selection artifact, not a discovery. The rhymes that ESCAPE the baseline are the real signal — e.g. the strong Cave↔4NT periagōgē ↔ tiparivaṭṭa ("a turning of the whole cognizer toward what-is, witnessed first-person before being taught"), an operation not a genre trait. Fix in flight: more families (the Jain flower coded S109) + threading the thin flowers off the T2/T5 plateau onto T1 + T9/T10.
A rhyme is a shared structural move across unlike traditions — same operation, different substrate + outcome. The orange box names how they DIFFER. The contact badge is a per-pair degree on a spectrum (rhyme is analogy, not sameness): no known direct contact (the strongest convergence signal) · plausible · indirect contact (known routes could carry it — Hellenistic diffusion, Indo-Greek) · documented contact (shared lineage/region, e.g. Hindu↔Buddhist). The less the contact, the more striking the shared move. Color = analogy kind. 'No known' ≠ 'none'; calls are family-level heuristics pending advisor confirmation (§13).

Forest cross-section — the chaos→homogeneity test (IRR119)

Cut all the flowers at one year. The single-flower thread reads down a lineage; this reads across the whole forest at a moment — drag the cut-plane and watch how many branches are alive and how dispersed they are. The Q2 hypothesis is that dispersion rises (proliferation/chaos) and falls (codification/homogeneity) at different eras. This is the TEST, not the claim: if a cut at a council/codification year shows no drop in dispersion, that's evidence against the hypothesis — we report it, we don't force it (§2.1 candidate; DeepSeek's falsifiability flag). 520 dated atoms across 14 flowers.
