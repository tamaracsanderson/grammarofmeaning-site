Grammar of Meaning · canonical · how the method is validated

The cohorts don't ratify the method. They change it.

A solo researcher has no rater pool. So the method is validated by putting the same question to five independent frontier models and reconciling where they agree, dissent, and where they overturn the instrument. Two rounds from a single day show what that actually buys.

1 · The problem this solves

Inter-rater reliability is the standard warrant in qualitative coding: if two trained humans reach the same code, the code isn't just one reader's projection. A solo MDiv researcher with no grant has no second rater, no coding team, no digital-humanities consultants. The honest options are: (a) code alone and claim reliability you can't demonstrate, (b) don't code, or (c) find a rater-substitute and be explicit about what it is and isn't.

We take (c). Five frontier models — GPT-5, Claude, Gemini 2.5 Pro, DeepSeek, Solar Pro — as independent cohorts. Not because they're equivalent to human experts (they aren't; see §5), but because they are genuinely independent of each other and of the method's author, and because disagreement between them is informative in exactly the way inter-rater disagreement is informative.

2 · The apparatus

1. A query file — the question, written once, with hard PASTE-START/PASTE-END markers so every cohort receives byte-identical input. No per-cohort tailoring.
2. Five cold cohorts — each answers with no knowledge of the others, and (critically) no knowledge of what the author hopes to hear.
3. Raw responses preserved — unedited, on disk, with provenance frontmatter. The reconciliation can always be audited against them.
4. A reconciliation doc — the deliverable. Findings sorted into UNANIMOUS / STRONG-MAJORITY / MAJORITY / SOLO, plus a verdict and action items. Chat-only summaries do not count; without the doc the round is unfinished.
5. A citation-veracity pass — every framework the cohorts name is checked. (Fabrication rates vary by cohort and by how dense the secondary literature is — that's a measured property, not an anecdote.)
Why SOLO findings are kept. A finding only one cohort raised is not noise to be averaged away. Several of the sharpest corrections in the two rounds below were SOLO — one model seeing something the other four missed. A method that only records consensus would have lost them.

3 · What it actually bought — two rounds, one day

IRR195 · the multiplicity instrument
5/5 told us to throw away the thing we'd just built

We had built a weighted multi-label coder: a figure holding two values on an axis gets a split — personal-agentive 60 / apophatic 40 — estimated from textual support. We'd fixed its bugs and were pleased with it.

All five cohorts independently said the unit is wrong. No coder — human or LLM — reliably discriminates 60/40 from 55/45; asking for it is response-granularity without evidential granularity. Use a calibrated coarse ordinal (Single · Lean · Dual) and derive the numbers downstream; the coder never emits a percentage.

They also split the frame in a way we hadn't: estimability is axis-dependent. Epistemic warrant and inferential operation are countable (a passage argues from revelation or reason). But ontology is architectonic — an apophatic qualifier governs every positive predicate, so "what percent is apophatic" is a category error, not a hard measurement. Those axes get sets, not ratios.

And one SOLO catch that reframed the whole thing: our sum-to-100 vector is a mixture, not graded set membership — which needn't sum to 1. A work can belong strongly to both sets. We had been silently conflating two different semantics.

IRR196 · the admissibility gate
5/5 build-with-changes — the architecture confirmed, the risk named

We had built a gate for counterfactual generation: is this imagined branch something the text's own era could have thought? Code the era on a fixed material substrate, derive a birthdate concept-lexicon, and gate each branch on era-availability × path-coherence.

The decomposition was confirmed — all five agreed the two predicates are the right cut, and that a fixed material substrate beats a named-era scheme (a named periodization "encodes the conclusion into the periodization" — the bins already describe the drift you'd use them to measure). Notably, all five independently re-derived our own substrate as the answer.

And they named the risk we'd have shipped: path-coherence is "not fuzzy — it is ungrounded." It must become a computed state-constraint (a move is coherent iff it shifts each coordinate axis by ≤1 step), not a holistic judgment. Left as intuition, its agreement drops below 0.50 and invalidates the gate.

Plus a fix we'd missed entirely: concept ≠ vocabulary. "Codependency" the phenomenon predates the 1980s term. Gate on when a concept was thinkable and sayable in this setting, not when it was named.

4 · The claim

A round that only ever confirms is a round that isn't measuring anything. In one day, five independent cohorts killed one instrument (the numeric weight), confirmed one architecture (the two gates), and named the failure mode we were about to ship (ungrounded coherence). That is the difference between validation and applause.

This is the project's methodological contribution, and it is why the corpus, the method, and the tooling are published rather than withheld: a method whose lineage you can audit is a method you can contest. The reconciliations are on disk with the raw responses beside them; anyone can re-run the query, get their own five answers, and show us where we're wrong.

There is a deeper result behind this. The same apparatus has demonstrated framing-dependence empirically: the same five cohorts, given the same engineering question under two different framings thirty minutes apart, returned unanimous opposite recommendations. Same models, same facts, different frame. That is not a curiosity — it is direct evidence for the bias-visibility commitment that governs the whole corpus: make the framing explicit, and the bias becomes auditable.

5 · What this is not

Honesty about the instrument's limits is what makes it usable:

LimitWhat we do about it
Not human expertsFive LLMs are a rater-substitute, not a scholar panel. They're strong on methodology (dense training literature), weaker where the secondary literature is thin. Named, not hidden.
Intra-model agreement inflatesA cheap same-model pre-check on our era-gate returned 10/10 unanimous — a useful sanity check, and not the real number. Cross-frontier is the defensible measure; the intra-model pass is explicitly labelled a pre-check.
Cohort-specific fabricationMeasured, not assumed: fabrication tracks how dense the English secondary literature is for the topic. One cohort is a judgment cohort, not a citation-retrieval cohort — and is used accordingly.
Consensus isn't truthSOLO findings are preserved; unanimity on a badly-framed question is worth nothing (see the framing-flip). The reconciliation records dissent, not just agreement.
And the standing limit: Stage 6 coding is at zero. These rounds validate the method. They do not produce findings — the corpus coding hasn't run. Every result downstream is a candidate until it does.
