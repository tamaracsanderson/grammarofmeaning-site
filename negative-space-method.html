Grammar of Meaning · canonical method

The negative-space method: reading what a text doesn't say — and what it couldn't have said

A reproducible procedure for locating a text's gaps, generating its un-taken alternatives, and gating both against what was thinkable in its own era. Worked end-to-end on a 1963 pop song and on Romans 8.

⚠️ Status: candidates, not findings. Stage 6 = 0. The corpus coding has not run. Everything below is a validated method producing candidate output — that distinction is load-bearing, and it is the honest position, not a hedge.

1 · Why negative space

What a tradition refuses to say is as structured as what it says. Romans 8:29 predestines but never fills the basis on which God foreknew — Paul raises the ground of election, the argument demands it, and he seals it. That is a reserved absence, not an oversight. The Buddha's silence on the fourteen unanswered questions looks identical on the surface — "no answer given" — but is a refusal: the question is set aside as not conducing to liberation. Same surface, different kind.

The method exists to answer one question: is attending to silence a hermeneutic that discriminates — telling traditions apart — rather than flattening them into the same ineffable mystery? The named risk is real and is the thing to defeat: a method that finds the same profound silence everywhere is projecting. So the governing rule is discrimination before convergence, and most of the apparatus below is guards.

2 · The procedure

step 1
Decompose each move on the CORE-7 grammar

A text is a sequence of moves. Each decomposes into seven slots:

Narrator[ Operationtype ( Agentvoice , Substrate ) → Outcome ]

operation (the verb — what the text does) · type (transformative/constitutive/deeming/yielding/report) · voice (active/middle-passive/null-stative) · agent (the in-frame originator) · substrate (what's acted on) · outcome (what's produced) · narrator (teller + POV + authority-source).

step 2
Find the gap: the 2×2, plus the seer

Each slot is placed on visibility (transparent ↔ opaque) × salience (out-of-focus ↔ in-focus). THE GAP is the opaque × in-focus cell. Then one further question — opaque to whom? — the seer: the agent (does she know it?), the narrator (can the teller vouch?), the reader (can we decode it?).

Why the seer matters: "She Loves You" is a friend relaying a woman's love. The form — a mediated report — structurally forecloses her first-person voice. The silence is produced by the architecture, not chosen by the author.

step 3
FILL vs SUBSTITUTE vs BARRED — and the frame-jump

FILL: "…AND X" co-holds — completes the move without retracting it (monotonic; stays in frame). SUBSTITUTE: "…X INSTEAD" — replaces the value, voiding the premise (non-monotonic; jumps frame). BARRED: foreclosed.

The distinction is load-bearing: within one seer's viewpoint you can only FILL. A substitute requires a different seer/Sitz — it is the door out of the frame into an adjacent one.

step 4
Resolutions ARE edges — and one runs backward

Each gap resolves three ways: filled-internally (a completion edge inside the text — and it can run backward: a later move filling an earlier gap is suspense) · generatable (the engine can produce a plausible in-frame fill) · RESIDUAL (neither the text nor a monotonic fill closes it — only reception can: a reception edge pointing out of the text, forward in history, where different readers fork).

The residuals are where the cross-tradition question lives. "She Loves You" turns out ~80% self-completing: most of its money-move's gaps are answered by its other moves via backward completion edges. The few it cannot fill from inside — the addressee's foreclosed reciprocity, the friend's motive — are exactly where history steps in.
step 5
The counterfactual: substitute one move, fan forward

Swap a single slot (M1 "you hurt her" → "you healed her") and the edge fans into the admissible next-moves, each of which fans again. Two views of one object: the fan (split at every fork — the whole space, ~3depth, undrawable past ~5 levels but perfectly holdable as a dataset) and a world-line (one coherent path — a single alternate song).

step 6
The two gates: era-availability × path-coherence

A branch is admissible only if it clears both:

ERA-AVAILABILITY — its governing concept existed. We code the era on a fixed 10-category material substrate (political-institutional · economic-material · communication-media · science-technology · epidemic-health · climate-ecology · demographic-social · migration-diaspora · interreligious-contact · kinship-residence) and derive a birthdate concept-lexicon. For 1963 romantic love: emotional dependency = Fromm 1956, attachment = Bowlby 1958, trapped-by-the-other = Sartre 1943, feminine discontent = Friedan Feb 1963 → in-frame. Versus self-actualisation as mass ideal (Maslow/Esalen, late-60s), enlightenment-as-romance-substitute (1968), codependency (1980s), "toxic relationship" (2000s) → barred.

PATH-COHERENCE — it must cohere with the world-line it descends from. Under "she is reliant on you and you enjoy it," the move "you release her freely from your own strength" is era-available (Fromm 1956) but world-incoherent — it belongs to a different world-line. A trunk is a coherence-constrained path.

Why a fixed material substrate, not a named-era scheme? A named periodization ("the Victorians," Küng's six paradigms) encodes the conclusion into the periodization — the bins already describe the drift they'd be used to measure. Coding the era on material conditions keeps the reconstruction auditable and the gate non-circular. Five independent labs converged on this same answer, re-deriving our own substrate.
step 7
Convergence: forward-fan ∩ backward-funnel

Run forward (what's reachable from the substitute?) and backward (which antecedents lead to the ending?) independently, and report only where they meet. They are different objects: the sung ending is a wide funnel backward (many antecedents fit) but a narrow target forward.

Worked: substituting "healed" for "hurt" removes the injury at the funnel's mouth, so a convergent path must re-introduce it — which is why only half the world-lines land on the sung apology, and why every one that does shares a single shape: healed → the healer withdraws → a new hurt → apology for the withdrawal.

This is the anti-teleology rule made mechanical: you do not invent a bridge to the ending because you know the ending. Convergence is a computed intersection, not a story you told.

3 · The thinkers we leaned on

None of the apparatus is invented from nothing. Each piece has a parent, and naming them is the point — a method whose lineage you can audit is a method you can contest.

Whose ideaWhat it gave us
GenetteFocalization & diegetic levels → the seer (opaque to whom — agent / narrator / reader), and the distinction between a move and a move reported.
FillmoreFrame semantics → the frame-roles: a term activates a frame with slots, and the unfilled slots are enumerable. The CORE-7 is a case-grammar of a move.
AikhenvaldEvidentiality → foreclosure: when warrant = witness, the first-person interior becomes structurally unreachable. The grammar produces the silence.
StalnakerPresupposition → the presupposition operator: absences that are unstated enabling conditions, not unfilled slots.
KuhnParadigm as community-level shared exemplars → the era-gate: what is thinkable is collective and time-indexed. (And why we rejected a progressive periodization: Kuhn's paradigms are incommensurable, not stages.)
KoselleckExperience/expectation as a transcendental pair → the Sitz as governor of the gap: material conditions set the size and rate of the expectable, which is exactly what our substrate codes.
Jauss & IserHorizon of expectations → the generative hinge: a gap that is obligated and shaped — a vector, not a hole.
RaginFuzzy-set calibration: a number between 0 and 1 means something only when its anchors are defined. This is why our multiplicity coding moved from invented percentages to a calibrated ordinal.

And the 1963 lexicon's own anchors — Sartre 1943 · Fromm 1956 · Bowlby 1958 · Friedan Feb 1963 — are not decoration: they are what make "in-frame" a falsifiable claim rather than a taste.

4 · How the method was tested

The method is validated the way it studies: by putting the same question to five independent frontier models and reconciling where they agree, disagree, and where they change our minds. Two rounds bear directly on the above.

On multiplicity (5/5): every cohort independently told us to drop our numeric weighted split — asking a coder for 60/40 vs 55/45 is precision the judgment can't support. Use a calibrated ordinal; derive the numbers downstream. They also split the frame: some axes (epistemic warrant, inferential operation) are prevalence-estimable; others (ontology, ground-world-relation) are architectonic — an apophatic qualifier governs every predicate, so asking "what percent" is a category error.

On the admissibility gate (5/5 build-with-changes): the two-gate decomposition was confirmed as the right one; the material-substrate + cited-lexicon was judged the right reproducibility strategy (it beats named-era, bespoke lists, and model intuition). And they named the real risks: path-coherence must become a computed state-constraint, not a holistic judgment; and the lexicon must distinguish a concept's name from its thinkability — "codependency" the phenomenon predates the 1980s term.

This is the methodological claim, and it is the honest one: the cohorts don't ratify the method — they change it. A round that only ever confirms is a round that isn't measuring anything.

5 · What is not claimed

Stage 6 coding stands at zero: no corpus finding is asserted here. Every generated branch is a thinking-tool, flagged as generated, that the actual text never takes. The gate is a pilot validated on a recent, densely-documented era (1963); it does not yet survive a hard era (50 AD, or a non-Western oral tradition) without confidence-tags, a refusal-to-guess rule, and the honest admission that one is gating on what the surviving sources preserve, not on what an era could think. Naming that boundary is what makes the rest usable.
