What a text is made of

One text goes in. We situate it, take it apart into the moves it's built from — what it says, and what it leaves unsaid — then recompose those moves four ways: as the alternatives it could have been, as commentary on its silences, as the readings history gave it, and as its variants across tellings. Each recomposition changes what it means.

Click any stage to see its fields — each definition, and whether the values are open (read off the text) or closed (from a controlled list, values defined). Fields we haven't defined yet show as ⚠ undefined.
