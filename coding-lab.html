The coding lab

The method behind the archive: the grammar, the schemas, the reliability runs — the negative-space instrument as it's actually built.

This is the substrate, not the public garden. The instrument reads what a text does (its moves) and what it conspicuously does not say (the gaps) — then asks which of those gaps history has to step in and fill. Below: the finalized reference, a live reliability test on a control text, and the raw process notes so you can see the thinking take shape.

Augustine changes his mind

32 years · his own Latin

The hardest test: point the instrument at a real historical mind and watch him reverse. On the same Romans 9 verse across three works and 32 years, it detects Augustine's flip from conditional to unconditional election — and finds it in two phases: the conviction changed at 396, but the door didn't harden to BARRED until 426. The grounding text, not the name, does the work.

Why is there suffering?

interactive · a coordinate answers

Fix one question every stance has a take on, then spin a coordinate — how it knows, what it takes to be real, how it moves, what it's for, its posture, its world-relation — and watch the six axes develop into an answer. The Augustinian reads Genesis 3; the apophatic un-says the question; a body-first seat no one occupies answers it as tension in tissue. Each answer names which axes did the work — that line is what a coordinate is. Candidate — not a finding.

What protects a founder's essence

interactive · 7 founders

Run the instrument across the rival portrayals of seven founders — Jesus, God, Muhammad, the Buddha, Confucius, Socrates, Freud. The essence axis holds when a single custodian or a genuinely shared core keeps it, and splits when rival custodians share none — the spread climbing 0.00 to 1.00. The Buddha is the exception that reveals the mechanism: fragmented custody, essence preserved. Custody, not sacredness, does the protecting.

The surprise axis — XT2

openness z = +1.6

Can the engine produce something you couldn't predict? A fourth axis (cognitive style) + a novelty metric from the creativity literature (surprise = associative distance). High-openness landed ~1.6 SD past the whole discipline spread on two encoders; high-neuroticism shifted content but not distance. The metric tells a leap from a content-shift.
