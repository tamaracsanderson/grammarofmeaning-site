The Method at Work · Demonstration 1

The same silences,
filled three ways

A famous passage leaves three questions unanswered. Watch three readers, eight centuries apart, walk up to the exact same gaps — and fill them differently.

This validates the instrument — that the gaps are really in the text, and that the method can sort who fills them. It is not a claim about who is right.  Scroll ↓

The text, and the gaps inside it

Paul writes this to Rome around 57 CE. Read the whole passage — the five moves (highlighted) are the verbs God does, in sequence; the three ◦ marks are the questions it raises and never answers.

Romans 8:28–30 (KJV) — “And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose. For whom he did foreknow◦ B2 · on what ground?, he also did predestinate to be conformed to the image of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brethren. Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called◦ B3 · where does the human act?: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.” ◦ B1 · and those he did not foreknow?

Five highlighted verbs = the golden chain. Three ◦ marks = the negative space — the questions the chain raises but never answers. (Full text in the KJV, public domain.)

Then — what it doesn't say

The chain is airtight about the sequence. But its own grammar raises three questions it never answers. These are the negative space — not gaps we invented, but questions the text itself opens and leaves open.

B2 · the basis
Foreknew on what ground?
It names the act — “those whom he foreknew” — but never its criterion. Foreknew what about them? Their faith? The love it just mentioned? Nothing they did at all?
B1 · the reprobate
And those he did not foreknow?
The chain describes only the included set. Naming a chosen group implies an un-chosen one — but the text says nothing about who they are, or what becomes of them.
B3 · the agency
Where does the human enter?
Verse 28 says the set “loves God” — a human act. Then every link is God's verb, the person only the object. The loving is never re-connected to the chain. Do we do anything?

Three obligated silences. Now watch what happens when serious readers meet them.

Three readers, eight centuries

They are not strangers to each other — each reads the two before him. But they read from different worlds (their Sitz — the when, where, and how-the-world-was that shapes a reading).

Augustine of Hippo
354–430 CE · Roman North Africa (Hippo, in modern Algeria) · bishop
Predestinarian. The late Augustine, arguing against Pelagius: grace is unearned and unstoppable; election rests on God's inscrutable will, not on anything he foresaw us do.
coded text: On Rebuke and Grace, c. 427
Thomas Aquinas
1225–1274 · Italy & Paris · Dominican friar, the great scholastic
The mediator. Grounds election in God's goodness (not foreseen merit, with Augustine) — but insists our free will is a real cause operating inside God's larger causing. Holds both.
coded text: Summa Theologiae I, q. 23, c. 1266
The Arminian
after Jacobus Arminius (d. 1609, Dutch) & the Remonstrants; carried by John Wesley & the Methodists
Synergist. God elects those he foreknows will freely believe; the will is a genuine, independent power; unconditional reprobation is rejected as a “horrible decree.”
coded text: an Arminian sermon in The Calvinistic Controversy, 1837 — the Wesleyan-Arminian line (not Wesley himself)

The chain names only the saved. Its silence is the whole God-subject chain — the un-chosen appear nowhere.

What just happened

Three readers met the same three silences and filled them differently. That two facts hold at once is the whole point:

— They found the same holes → the holes belong to the text, not to any one reader's bias.

— They filled them differently → the map sorts the readers who fight over them.

And it sorts more than where they stand. It shows the shape of the dispute: the ground (B2) is nearly categorical — you're basically predestinarian or you're not — while the mechanisms (B1, B3) open real spectra, room for a mediator like Aquinas to stand in the middle. The negative-space map tells you which questions are either/or and which are a matter of degree.

Sorted, not scored. The instrument places the readers; it never says which fill is correct. And it validates the method, not any claim about religion — these are candidates, not findings; no passage is coded yet (Stage-6 coding = 0).
