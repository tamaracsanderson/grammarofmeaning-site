Reception discrimination — a map of one verse

The instrument reads a text for its negative space — the questions the words leave open — then lays out how different theologies fill them. Below is one verse as a navigable map: the verse runs down the left as a labeled axis, each open question is a numbered station, and each reader has a fixed place. Follow it top to bottom; nothing is assumed. A method note, not an apology: we measure the in-between; we don't fill it. The coding here is a candidate, not a finding — no passages are finalized yet. What the map shows is the instrument working: that it locates real holes and sorts the fills.

1 Basis of election · hole B2

On what ground is anyone "foreknown"? The chain starts at foreknew but never says why.

AUGUSTINE

AQUINAS
God's goodness resolving into sheer divine will; rejects foreseen-merits
ARMINIAN
foreknowledge of a foreseen response, made prior to predestination

Two clustered, one apart. Augustine and Aquinas sit almost on top of each other; the Arminian reading is off on its own. This hole is a fork, not a gradient — you are on one side or the other.

2 Reprobation · hole B1

What of those not chosen? The chain names only the saved; it stays silent about the rest.

3 Where agency enters · hole B3

Every verb is God's; the human is only the object. So where — if anywhere — does the human act?

AUGUSTINE
absorbed into God's purpose (perseverance itself a gift)
AQUINAS
double-causality — free will a real secondary cause, contained under God as first cause
ARMINIAN

Three spread across. Same gradient shape as station 2 — the human's agency is contested by degree, not by a yes/no. Compare with station 1: the map sorts not just where readers stand but the shape of the dispute.

Read across all three stations: the map sorts not only where each reader stands but the shape of each dispute — station 1 categorical (a fork), stations 2 and 3 gradient (a spread). Sorted, not scored: no fill is marked correct; the dots sit on an axis, never ranked.
