Augustine changes his mind — in his own Latin, across 32 years

The instrument was built on a control text and transferred to scripture. This is the hardest test yet: point it at a real historical mind — the same author, the same verse, three works, 32 years apart — and ask whether it can watch him change. It could. And it found the change had a shape no two-point read would catch.

The gap — the same seam as Romans 8

At Romans 9:11–13 (Jacob and Esau, "the elder shall serve the younger"), Paul grounds election before the twins have done anything — raising the gap the whole doctrine splits on: is election grounded in foreseen faith (conditional) or in God's gratuitous purpose (unconditional)?

"Iacob dilexi, Esau autem odio habui" — Jacob I loved, but Esau I hated. The verse (Rom 9:13) Augustine reads in Ad Simplicianum.

We grounded the frame in Augustine's own Latin at this one locus, in three works written across his career — and read the election-ground and the door (can the rival reading still be posed?) each time. No name-priors: the signal is his sentences, not "reason, as Augustine."

The trajectory — three works, 32 years

c. 394 · the young priest
Expositio quarundam propositionum §52
groundconditional 3/3doorFILL 3/3
Election is grounded in foreseen faith; free will stands, the door is open. The young Augustine even raises the free-will worry in his own words — that some fear Paul "took away the free choice of the will."
396 · the pivot
Ad Simplicianum §2.18
groundunconditional 3/3doormixed — wrestling
The ground flips: election is now God's gratuitous purpose, not foreseen faith. But the door is unstable — SUBSTITUTE, FILL and BARRED all appear in the same text. This is Augustine visibly working it out. His conviction has changed; his confidence has not.
426 · the old bishop
De correptione et gratia
groundunconditional 3/3doorBARRED 2/3
Ground still unconditional; now the door hardens to BARRED — the conditional reading has become unaskable, no referent left to pose it. Thirty years on, what was a wrestling is now dogma.

The finding — a reversal in two phases

phase 1 · the ground flips

Conviction, at the pivot

394 conditional → 396 unconditional, and it stays flipped. This is the reversal every Augustine scholar knows — Ad Simplicianum is the hinge of his anti-Pelagian turn. The instrument didn't invent it; it found it, from the Latin.

phase 2 · the door hardens

Confidence, thirty years later

The door settles to BARRED only by 426 — long after the ground flipped. The conviction changed before the confidence did. A two-point contrast would have seen one flip; the instrument saw its shape.

Why this is the strongest validation yet. It is a real, independently attested reversal — not a model's tidy answer. And it cuts the right way: grounding the frame in the early text pulls it to conditional, grounding in the late text pulls it to unconditional — so the grounding text, not the name, is doing the work (exactly what a corpus-first method demands). Asked abstractly, "model-Augustine" gives the settled answer; the texts give the untidy real trajectory. The human was less tidy than the model — because he changed his mind.

The refinement it surfaced

The coarse coordinate is likely stable across the whole reversal — Augustine stays revelation × transcendent-personal × exegesis his whole life. The change lives one level down, at the election-ground / door grain — which is the coordinate×gap finding. So a figure's trajectory is best read as a sequence of doors on one gap, not (only) a walk through the five axes. The atlas records Augustine as two per-work rows on one figure — the schema's first real "same mind, two works" case.

What's next

This is N=3 per work — the opening trajectory. Ahead: the door nuance at 426 read closely (is mature Augustine barring the conditional or substituting it?) at N≥5; XT-verification of both atlas rows; and the full Latin-native run once the late works (De correptione, Ad Simplicianum in full) are in the corpus. The trajectory is a candidate the instrument is built to test — not a finished finding.
